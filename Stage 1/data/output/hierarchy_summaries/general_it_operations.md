The classification of diabetes type is driven by clinical presentation and etiology rather than chronological age alone, requiring careful differentiation between Type 1 and Type 2 even in adult patients. While Type 1 diabetes is historically associated with juvenile onset, it can manifest at any age, making age thresholds insufficient for accurate diagnosis. Data quality checks must validate age against clinical diagnosis codes to prevent misclassification risks.

**Key Features:**
- Age is not the sole determining factor for classifying diabetes type
- Most Type 1 diabetics develop the condition before reaching puberty
- Type 1 diabetes mellitus is also referred to as juvenile diabetes

- Type 1 diabetes can manifest in adults despite the 'juvenile' moniker

- Puberty status is a common but not exclusive marker for onset timing
