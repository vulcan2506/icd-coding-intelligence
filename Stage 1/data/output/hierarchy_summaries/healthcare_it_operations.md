# Healthcare IT Operations

This topic ensures accurate chronic disease management by capturing detailed medication histories for patients on long-term insulin and oral hypoglycemic therapies. It distinguishes between therapy durations, validates dosage consistency, and flags potential drug interactions to support longitudinal care.

**Key Features:**
- Long-term Insulin Usage Tracking records the duration and specific type of insulin therapy for diabetic patients.
- Oral Hypoglycemic Medication Logging captures specific oral agents and their long-term administration status.

**Notable Details:**
- Reference section I.C.4.a.3 for detailed clinical guidelines.
- Data must distinguish between short-term and long-term therapy durations.
- Includes specific drug class categorization for both insulin and oral agents.
- Supports integration with electronic health records for longitudinal care.
- Requires validation of dosage consistency over time.
- Flags potential drug interactions between insulin and oral hypoglycemics.
- Ensures patient education records are linked to medication adherence.
