# Clinical Coding Standards

These guidelines establish a rigorous framework for sequencing and assigning principal diagnoses in complex clinical scenarios, prioritizing the site of active treatment or the primary malignancy based on clinical intent. They ensure accurate classification of maternal, fetal, and neoplasm-related conditions by distinguishing between pre-existing and pregnancy-related issues, managing metastatic disease, and adhering to specific coding protocols for high-risk situations.

**Key Features:**
- Pregnancy Complication Coding differentiates pre-existing conditions from those developing due to pregnancy to assign correct codes.
- Trimester Coding Guidelines assigns the final character based on provider documentation of trimester or weeks for both pre-existing and new conditions.
- Newborn Examination Coding codes all clinically significant conditions while assigning prematurity codes only when explicitly documented.
- Pregnancy Substance Use Coding assigns specific O99.31, O99.33, and O99.32 codes for alcohol, tobacco, and drug use during pregnancy or postpartum.
- Perinatal Condition Coding defines the perinatal period as before birth through day 28, requiring conditions to originate in this period for coding.
- Pregnancy Complication Diagnosis Sequencing sequences the principal complication first in non-delivery episodes and the admission-prompting condition if delivery occurs.
- ICD-10 Maternal Fetal Care Coding requires 7th character assignment to identify the affected fetus in specific complication categories.
- HIV Pregnancy Diagnosis Coding sequences O98.7- codes before specific HIV illness codes for patients admitted due to HIV-related illness.
- Gestational Diabetes Code Subcategory O24.4 restricts gestational diabetes coding to subcategory O24.4, excluding other O24 codes.
- Fetal Surgery Coding Guidelines assigns category O35 codes for fetal conditions and prohibits using Chapter 16 codes on the mother's record.
- High-Risk Pregnancy Coding Guidelines uses category O09 only for the prenatal period and complication codes for labor/delivery issues.
- Diabetes in Pregnancy Coding Protocol sequences category O24 codes before Chapter 4 diabetes codes for pregnant patients with diabetes.
- Congenital Malformation Coding Guidelines assigns Q00-Q99 codes for malformations, allowing them as principal or secondary diagnoses.
- Perinatal Condition Continuity mandates continued use of perinatal codes for conditions originating in the perinatal period regardless of patient age.
- Principal Diagnosis Assignment for Malignancy designates the primary malignancy as principal when treatment targets the primary site, or Z51.- codes when treatment is chiefly chemotherapy or radiation.

**Notable Details:**
- Assign two P07 codes when both birth weight and estimated gestational age are available.
- Sequence abuse codes (O9A.3, O9A.4, O9A.5) first for suspected or confirmed abuse cases.
- Assign 7th character '0' for single gestations or when documentation is insufficient.

### Neoplasm Coding Guidelines

These guidelines establish a comprehensive framework for sequencing and assigning principal diagnoses in neoplasm encounters, prioritizing the site of active treatment or the primary malignancy based on clinical intent. They provide specific rules for complex scenarios involving metastatic disease, chemotherapy, pregnancy, and transplanted organs to ensure accurate coding.

**Key Features:**
- Principal Diagnosis Assignment for Malignancy: Designates the primary malignancy as principal when treatment targets the primary site, or Z51.- codes when treatment is chiefly chemotherapy or radiation.
- Primary Malignancy Coding Guidelines: Requires sequencing the primary malignancy code until treatment is completed if excised but further treatment is directed to that site.
- Neoplasm and Anemia Encounter Coding Guidelines: Sequences malignancy code first followed by anemia code D63.0 when admission is solely for managing anemia associated with malignancy.
- Tumor Coding Clarification Protocol: Assigns codes for multiple tumors based on whether they represent different primaries or metastatic disease, requiring provider queries for unclear documentation.
- C80.0 Unspecified Neoplasm Use Case: Reserved exclusively for advanced metastatic disease with no known primary or secondary sites specified.
- Extranodal Lymphoma Coding Rules: Assigns codes from categories C81-C85 with a final character for extranodal sites and prohibits assigning a secondary neoplasm code for the affected solid organ.
- Secondary Neoplasm Principal Diagnosis Logic: Designates the secondary neoplasm as principal diagnosis when treatment is directed toward the secondary site only, even if the primary is present.
- Neoplasm Coding and Diagnosis Guidelines: Designates the complication as the principal diagnosis when an encounter is for treatment of a complication resulting from a surgical procedure for the neoplasm.
- Metastatic Site Principal Diagnosis Coding: Designates the metastatic site as principal diagnosis when treatment is directed toward it only, with the primary malignancy coded additionally.
- Malignant Neoplasm Transplant Coding Protocol: Codes malignant neoplasm of a transplanted organ as a transplant complication using category T86.- followed by C80.2.
- Malignant Neoplasm Sequencing for Pregnant Patients: Sequences code from subcategory O9A.1- first for pregnant patients with malignant neoplasm complicating pregnancy.
- Malignancy Diagnosis Coding Rules: Designates primary malignancy or metastatic site as principal diagnosis when the reason for admission is to determine the extent of malignancy.
- Neoplasm Episode and Chemotherapy Coding Guidelines: Assigns neoplasm code as principal when surgical removal is followed by adjunct chemotherapy or radiation in the same episode.

**Notable Details:**

### ICD-10 Coding Guidelines

These guidelines establish standardized protocols for selecting and sequencing ICD-10 codes across various clinical scenarios, including outpatient diagnoses, transplant complications, and preventive care. They emphasize the precedence of specific disease guidelines over general conventions and define strict rules for the use of Z codes as primary or secondary diagnoses based on encounter circumstances.

**Key Features:**
- Outpatient First-Listed Diagnosis Coding: Assign the primary diagnosis based on the reason for the visit using the Alphabetic Index first.
- Kidney Transplant Complication Coding: Assign T86.1 only for documented complications like rejection or failure, excluding chronic kidney disease.
- HIV Medication Coding: Use B20 for HIV disease status and Z79.899 for long-term antiretroviral medication use.
- Z Code Usage Guidelines: Select Z codes as first-listed or secondary based on specific encounter circumstances and chapter-specific rules.
- CKD Post-Transplant Coding: Assign N18 codes for CKD stage and Z94.0 for transplant status without coding CKD as a complication unless specified.
- Z28 Underimmunization Code Mapping: Map Z28 encounters to Z29, Z40, or Z41 while excluding Z28.3 from these mappings.
- Z13 Screening Encounter Classification: Use screening codes as first-listed when the visit is specifically for early disease detection in well individuals.
- Z05 Secondary Coding for Z38: Assign Z05 codes as secondary diagnoses following Z38 codes for liveborn infants with additional conditions.
- Z Code Procedure Code Requirements: Require a corresponding procedure code to accompany any Z code describing a performed procedure.
- HIV Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis Coding: Assign Z29.81 for encounters involving the administration of HIV pre-exposure prophylaxis medication.
- Diagnosis Code Identification Requirements: Select diagnosis codes strictly from ranges A00.0 through T88.9, Z00-Z99, and U00-U85.
- Counseling Z Code Mapping Updates: Map general counseling encounters to specific codes like Z31.5 for procreative genetic counseling.
- Encounter Codes for Abnormal Findings: Use codes for general medical examinations with abnormal findings as the first-listed diagnosis when new conditions are identified.

**Notable Details:**
- The search for correct code assignment must begin through the Alphabetic Index, never the Tabular List initially.
- Do not assign T86.1- for post-transplant patients with chronic kidney disease unless a specific transplant complication is documented.
- Z codes are not procedure codes and must be accompanied by a corresponding procedure code if a procedure was performed.
- Z28.3 (Underimmunization status) is explicitly excluded from mapping to Z29, Z40, and Z41.

### Edge Cases & Standalone Topics

These guidelines establish strict sequencing and assignment rules for complex clinical scenarios involving neoplasms, congenital anomalies, obstetrical conditions, and adverse effects. They prioritize the primary focus of treatment while defining specific temporal windows and exclusion criteria for accurate coding.

**Key Features:**
- Pathological Fracture Coding Sequencing: Prioritizes M84.5 or neoplasm codes based on whether the fracture or the underlying cancer is the primary treatment focus.
- Congenital Anomaly Exemption Logic: Assigns 'Y' for congenital conditions except those in categories Q00-Q99, which are exempt from this assignment.
- Intrauterine and Delivery Condition Adjudication: Treats conditions present at birth or developed in utero as present on admission, while delivery-specific events are assigned 'Y'.
- POA Assignment Logic for Obstetrical Conditions: Determines Present on Admission status based on the presence of the pregnancy complication at admission, regardless of delivery timing.
- Postpartum Complication Definition: Classifies complications occurring within the six-week period following delivery as postpartum.
- Peripartum and Postpartum Period Definitions: Defines the postpartum period as starting immediately after delivery and lasting six weeks, and the peripartum period as the last month of pregnancy to five months postpartum.
- tPA Administration Exclusion Criteria: Excludes Z92.82 only if tPA was administered in a different facility within 24 hours prior to admission.
- Malignancy Principal Diagnosis and Prophylactic Organ Removal Rules: Prevents symptoms or signs associated with malignancy from replacing the malignancy as the principal diagnosis.
- Chapter 16 Coding Guidelines: Allows Chapter 16 codes to be sequenced first for perinatal conditions and permits other chapter codes for added specificity.
- O9A Sequencing for Toxic Effects: Requires subcategory O9A.2 codes to be sequenced first, followed by specific injury or toxic effect codes.
- Anemia Sequencing for Chemotherapy Adverse Effects: Mandates sequencing anemia codes first when admission is solely for managing anemia resulting from chemotherapy or radiotherapy.

**Notable Details:**
- Categories Q00-Q99 are explicitly exempt from the congenital anomaly 'Y' assignment logic.
- Newborns are not considered admitted until after birth, yet conditions present at birth are treated as present on admission.
- The tPA exclusion rule strictly limits the 24-hour window to administrations occurring in a different facility.
- Symptoms and signs associated with an existing malignancy cannot serve as the principal diagnosis even with multiple admissions.
- For perinatal conditions, Chapter 16 codes take precedence in sequencing over other chapter codes.
- The peripartum period extends from the last month of pregnancy through five months postpartum.
