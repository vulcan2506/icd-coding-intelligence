# POA Indicator Assignment Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** POA Indicator Assignment and Exemptions

**Key Behaviors:**
- The POA indicator is assigned to diagnosis and external cause codes after ICD-10-CM codes are assigned in accordance with official coding guidelines.
- Medical record documentation from any provider legally accountable for establishing the patient's diagnosis may be used to support the determination of POA status.
- The provider must be queried regarding issues related to the linking of signs/symptoms, timing of test results, and the timing of findings.
- Codes and categories on the CDC exempt list do not require the use of a POA indicator as they represent circumstances or factors that do not represent a current disease or injury.
- The guidelines supplement the main body of ICD-10-CM Official Guidelines but do not replace them or provide guidance on when a condition should be coded.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- A joint effort between the healthcare provider and the coder is essential to achieve complete and accurate documentation, code assignment, and reporting.
- The provider must be legally accountable for establishing the patient's diagnosis for their documentation to be used in POA determination.
- The POA indicator must be assigned to conditions that have been coded following the assignment of ICD-10-CM codes.
- The provider's clinical judgment regarding whether a condition was present on admission is the primary determinant, not the guidelines themselves.
- Documentation must be consistent and complete to support the determination of whether a condition was present on admission.
- The guidelines explicitly reference the CDC website as the authoritative source for the detailed list of exempt ICD-10-CM codes.
- The term 'provider' is legally defined as a physician or any qualified healthcare practitioner who is legally accountable for establishing the patient's diagnosis.

**New in this version:**
- The guidelines explicitly reference the CDC website for the detailed list of ICD-10-CM codes that do not require the use of a POA indicator.
