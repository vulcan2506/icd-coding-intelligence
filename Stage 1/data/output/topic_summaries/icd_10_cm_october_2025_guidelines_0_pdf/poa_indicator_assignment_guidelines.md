# POA Indicator Assignment Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** POA Indicator Assignment and Exemptions

**Key Behaviors:**
- POA guidelines supplement main ICD-10-CM guidelines to assign indicators to already assigned diagnosis and external cause codes.
- Medical record documentation from any legally accountable provider may be used to support the determination of POA status.
- Providers must be queried regarding issues related to linking signs/symptoms, timing of test results, and timing of findings.
- Codes and categories on the CDC exempt list do not require the use of a POA indicator.
- Exempt codes describe circumstances regarding the healthcare encounter or factors influencing health status that do not represent a current disease or injury.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- POA indicator assignment occurs only after ICD-10-CM codes have been assigned in accordance with Sections I, II, and III of the official coding guidelines.
- The provider must be legally accountable for establishing the patient's diagnosis to support the determination of POA status.
- Provider clinical judgment is required to determine whether a condition was present on admission.
- Documentation must be consistent and complete to achieve accurate code assignment and reporting.
- The CDC website must be consulted for the detailed list of ICD-10-CM codes exempt from POA indicator requirements.
- The POA guidelines are not intended to provide guidance on when a condition should be coded.
- The POA indicator should be assigned to conditions only after they have been coded in accordance with Sections I, II, and III of the official coding guidelines.
