# ICD-10-CM Z Code Reporting Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Z Code and External Cause Reporting Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Z codes may be assigned as appropriate to further explain reasons for healthcare encounters, including transfers between facilities.
- Z codes and external cause codes are not an all-inclusive list; other applicable codes may be assigned based on documentation.
- Assign as many codes as necessary to fully explain each healthcare encounter.
- Use any available documentation to assign appropriate external cause of morbidity and Z codes when patient history is limited.
- Z codes may be assigned to explain reasons for presenting for healthcare services, including transfers between healthcare facilities.
- The guidelines explicitly list specific Z codes (e.g., Z59.0, Z59.1, Z59.5, Z75.1, Z75.3, Z75.4, Z76.2, Z99.12) as examples of applicable codes.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Codes must be assigned based on the specific circumstances to determine if they are principal/first-listed or secondary.
- Documentation must support the assignment of the specific Z code or external cause code.
- Codes must be assigned as principal/first-listed diagnosis only, secondary diagnosis only, or principal/first-listed or secondary depending on the specific circumstances.
