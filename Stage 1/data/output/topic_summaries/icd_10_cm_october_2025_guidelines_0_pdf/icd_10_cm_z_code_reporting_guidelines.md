# ICD-10-CM Z Code Reporting Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10-CM Z Code Reporting Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Z codes may be assigned as appropriate to further explain reasons for healthcare services, including transfers between facilities.
- Assign as many codes as necessary to fully explain each healthcare encounter.
- Use any available documentation to assign appropriate external cause of morbidity and Z codes when patient history is limited.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Codes must be assigned based on whether they are designated as principal/first-listed diagnosis only, secondary diagnosis only, or both depending on circumstances.
- Documentation must support the assignment of specific Z codes such as homelessness (Z59.0-), inadequate housing (Z59.1), or extreme poverty (Z59.5).
- External cause of morbidity codes and Z codes are not an all-inclusive list; other applicable codes must be used based on documentation.

**New in this version:**
- Specific inclusion of Z75.1 for persons awaiting admission to adequate facilities elsewhere.
- Inclusion of Z99.12 for encounters related to respirator dependence during power failure.
- Explicit guidance to utilize available documentation for external cause and Z code assignment when patient history is very limited.
- Inclusion of Z75.3 for unavailability and inaccessibility of health-care facilities.
- Inclusion of Z75.4 for unavailability and inaccessibility of other helping agencies.
- Inclusion of Z76.2 for encounter for health supervision and care of other healthy infant and child.
