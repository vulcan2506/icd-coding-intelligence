# Influenza Coding Guidelines for Confirmed Cases
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Influenza Coding for Confirmed Cases

**Key Behaviors:**
- Code only confirmed cases of influenza due to certain identified influenza viruses (category J09) and due to other identified influenza virus (category J10).
- Coding is based on the provider's diagnostic statement of a specific strain (e.g., H1N1, H3N2) rather than requiring positive laboratory testing documentation.
- If the provider records 'suspected', 'possible', or 'probable' influenza, assign category J11 (Influenza due to unidentified influenza virus) instead of J09 or J10.
- Category J09 and J10 codes must not be assigned if the diagnosis is documented as uncertain or unconfirmed.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Provider must explicitly document a specific identified influenza virus or strain.
- Diagnosis must be confirmed by the provider's statement; laboratory test results are not strictly required for confirmation.
- Uncertain diagnoses (suspected, possible, probable) require assignment of category J11.
