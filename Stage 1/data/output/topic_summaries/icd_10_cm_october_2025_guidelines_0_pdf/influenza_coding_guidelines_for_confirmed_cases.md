# Influenza Coding Guidelines for Confirmed Cases
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Influenza Coding for Confirmed Cases

**Key Behaviors:**
- Code only confirmed cases of influenza due to certain identified influenza viruses (category J09) and due to other identified influenza virus (category J10).
- Coding is based on the provider's diagnostic statement regarding specific strains (e.g., avian, H1N1, H3N2) rather than requiring positive laboratory testing documentation.
- If the provider records 'suspected', 'possible', or 'probable' influenza, assign category J11 (Influenza due to unidentified influenza virus) instead of J09 or J10.
- Category J09 codes must not be assigned for suspected or probable cases of avian or novel influenza.
- Category J10 codes must not be assigned for suspected or probable cases of other identified influenza strains.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The provider must make a definitive diagnostic statement identifying the specific influenza strain or type.
- Documentation must explicitly rule out 'suspected', 'possible', or 'probable' status to utilize categories J09 or J10.
- For uncertain diagnoses where specific strain confirmation is absent, category J11 is the mandatory assignment.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Assigning category J09 or J10 codes when the diagnosis is documented only as 'suspected', 'possible', or 'probable'.
- Relying on positive laboratory testing documentation as a strict prerequisite for assigning confirmed influenza codes when a provider statement exists.
- Using category J09 or J10 for cases where the influenza virus is unidentified or the diagnosis remains uncertain.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit exception to the hospital inpatient guideline Section II, H. (Uncertain Diagnosis) allowing coding of confirmed influenza without lab test documentation.
- Mandatory use of category J11 for any influenza diagnosis lacking definitive confirmation of a specific identified virus.
