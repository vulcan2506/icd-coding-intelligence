# ICD-10-CM Principal Diagnosis Sequencing
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Etiology/Manifestation Sequencing and Interrelated Conditions

**Key Behaviors:**
- Underlying etiology codes must be sequenced first, followed by manifestation codes when a combination exists.
- Manifestation codes titled 'in diseases classified elsewhere' are never permitted as first-listed or principal diagnosis codes.
- Alphabetic Index entries listing etiology followed by manifestation codes in brackets indicate the bracketed code must be sequenced second.
- For interrelated conditions potentially meeting principal diagnosis definition, either may be sequenced first unless specific circumstances indicate otherwise.
- 'Code first' and 'Use additional code' notes serve as sequencing rules for both etiology/manifestation combinations and other specific codes.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Manifestation codes must be listed following the underlying condition code.
- Codes in brackets within Alphabetic Index entries must always be sequenced second.
- Principal diagnosis sequencing must align with circumstances of admission, therapy provided, Tabular List, or Alphabetic Index instructions.
- Etiology codes must be used in conjunction with manifestation codes for 'in diseases classified elsewhere' entries.
- Instructional notes ('code first', 'use additional code') must be followed to determine proper sequencing order.
- Codes with 'in diseases classified elsewhere' in the title must be used in conjunction with an underlying condition code.
