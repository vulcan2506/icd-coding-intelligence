# Pregnancy Substance Use Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Pregnancy Substance Use and Abuse Coding

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign subcategory O99.31 for alcohol use during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium with a secondary F10 code.
- Assign subcategory O99.33 for tobacco use during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium with a secondary F17 code.
- Assign subcategory O99.32 for drug use (illegal or prescription abuse) during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium with secondary F11-F16/F18-F19 codes.
- Sequence abuse codes (O9A.3, O9A.4, O9A.5) first for suspected or confirmed abuse cases, followed by injury and perpetrator codes.
- Identify manifestations of substance use by assigning secondary codes from specific F categories alongside the pregnancy complication codes.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must confirm substance use occurred during pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium period.
- Secondary codes from categories F10, F17, F11-F16, or F18-F19 must be assigned to identify specific manifestations.
- For abuse cases, documentation must support the specific type of abuse (physical, sexual, psychological) to sequence O9A codes first.
- Injury codes and perpetrator codes must be assigned if associated with confirmed abuse cases.
- Coding must distinguish between illegal drug use and inappropriate use/abuse of prescription drugs under O99.32.
- Documentation must confirm the patient uses any type of tobacco product during pregnancy or postpartum to assign O99.33.
- Documentation must confirm the patient uses drugs (illegal or prescription abuse) during pregnancy or postpartum to assign O99.32.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory assignment of secondary codes from F categories to identify specific substance use manifestations.
- Explicit sequencing rule requiring abuse codes (O9A.3-O9A.5) to be sequenced first before associated injury or perpetrator codes.
- Inclusion of psychological abuse (O9A.5) as a distinct category requiring specific sequencing alongside physical and sexual abuse.
- Explicit inclusion of 'inappropriate use or abuse of prescription drugs' within the drug use complicating pregnancy category (O99.32).
- Mandatory sequencing of abuse codes (O9A.3, O9A.4, O9A.5) as the principal diagnosis before associated injury or perpetrator codes.
- Recognition of psychological abuse (O9A.5) as a distinct category requiring specific sequencing alongside physical and sexual abuse.
