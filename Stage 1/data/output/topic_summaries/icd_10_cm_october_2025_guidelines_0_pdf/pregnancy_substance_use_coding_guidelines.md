# Pregnancy Substance Use Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Pregnancy Substance Use Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign O99.31 codes for alcohol use during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium regardless of expectedness.
- Assign O99.33 codes for tobacco use during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium regardless of expectedness.
- Assign O99.32 codes for drug use (illegal or inappropriate prescription) during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium.
- Sequence abuse codes (O9A.3, O9A.4, O9A.5) first for suspected or confirmed abuse cases, followed by injury and perpetrator codes.
- Always assign secondary codes from categories F10, F17, F11-F16, or F18-F19 to identify manifestations of substance use.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Documentation must indicate patient use of alcohol, tobacco, or drugs during pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium.
- Secondary codes from specific F categories must be assigned to identify manifestations of the substance use.
- For abuse cases, documentation must indicate suspected or confirmed abuse to sequence O9A codes first.
- Secondary codes must identify the type of nicotine dependence or drug use manifestations.
- Associated current injury and perpetrator codes must be assigned if applicable in abuse cases.
- Secondary codes from category F17 must be assigned to identify the specific type of nicotine dependence.
- Secondary codes from categories F11-F16 and F18-F19 must be assigned to identify manifestations of drug use.
