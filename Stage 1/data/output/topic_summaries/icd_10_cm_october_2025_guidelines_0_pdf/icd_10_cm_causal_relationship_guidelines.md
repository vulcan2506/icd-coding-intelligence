# ICD-10-CM Causal Relationship Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10-CM Causal Relationship Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Interpret 'with' or 'in' in code titles or instructional notes to mean 'associated with' or 'due to'.
- Presume a causal relationship between conditions linked by these terms in the Alphabetic Index or Tabular List.
- Code conditions as related even without explicit provider documentation unless documentation clearly states they are unrelated.
- Follow specific guidelines requiring documented linkage (e.g., sepsis) when general presumption does not apply.
- Recognize that 'with' in the Alphabetic Index is sequenced immediately following the main term, not alphabetically.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Provider documentation must explicitly link conditions if a specific guideline requires documented linkage.
- Documentation must clearly state conditions are unrelated to override the presumption of causality.
- Conditions must be linked by relational terms ('with', 'in') in the classification or specific guidelines to be coded as related.
- Conditions must be coded as related unless documentation clearly states they are unrelated or another guideline requires documented linkage.
