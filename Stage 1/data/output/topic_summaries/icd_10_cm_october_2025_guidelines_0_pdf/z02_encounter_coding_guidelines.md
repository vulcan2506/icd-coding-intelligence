# Z02 Encounter Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Z02 Encounter Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes are used for routine examinations like general check-ups or administrative purposes such as pre-employment physicals.
- Codes must not be used if the examination is for diagnosing a suspected condition or for treatment purposes.
- If a diagnosis or condition is discovered during a routine exam, it must be coded as an additional code.
- Pre-existing and chronic conditions may be included as additional codes if the exam is for administrative purposes and not focused on a specific condition.
- Code assignment for 'with' vs 'without' abnormal findings depends on information known at the time of coding.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The examination must be for administrative purposes and not focused on any particular condition.
- No treatment should be given for pre-operative or pre-procedural laboratory examination Z codes.
- Additional codes must be assigned to identify specific abnormal findings when using 'with abnormal findings' codes.
- Diagnosis codes must be used instead of Z codes if the exam is for diagnosis of a suspected condition.
- Code assignment must reflect the information known at the time the encounter is being coded.
- Pre-operative examination and pre-procedural laboratory examination Z codes are restricted to situations where a patient is cleared for a procedure or surgery and no treatment is given.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Using Z codes when the examination is for diagnosis of a suspected condition.
- Using Z codes when the examination is for treatment purposes.
- Coding a discovered diagnosis as the primary code instead of an additional code during a routine exam.
- Assigning 'with abnormal findings' codes without additional codes to identify specific findings.
- Using Z02 codes for examinations focused on a particular condition rather than administrative purposes.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit distinction between 'with' and 'without' abnormal findings in code assignment.
- Requirement to code discovered conditions as additional codes during routine exams.
- Inclusion of pre-existing and chronic conditions as additional codes for administrative exams.
- Specific exclusion of Z02.9 for unspecified administrative examinations.
- Mandatory use of diagnosis codes over Z codes for suspected conditions or treatment purposes.
- Explicit exclusion of Z02.9 for unspecified administrative examinations.
