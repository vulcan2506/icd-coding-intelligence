# Z02 Encounter Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Z02 Encounter Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Z codes are used for routine examinations like general check-ups or administrative purposes such as pre-employment physicals.
- Z codes must not be used if the examination is for diagnosing a suspected condition or for treatment purposes; in those cases, the diagnosis code is used.
- If a diagnosis or condition is discovered during a routine exam, it should be coded as an additional code.
- Pre-existing and chronic conditions and history codes may be included as additional codes if the exam is for administrative purposes and not focused on a particular condition.
- Code assignment for 'with' vs 'without' abnormal findings depends on information known at the time of coding; if results are pending, 'without abnormal findings' is acceptable.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- The examination must be for routine or administrative purposes, not for diagnosis of a suspected condition.
- The examination must not be for treatment purposes.
- If abnormal findings are present, additional codes must be assigned to identify the specific findings.
- Pre-operative and pre-procedural laboratory Z codes require that no treatment be given and the patient is being cleared for a procedure.
- History codes may only be included if the examination is not focused on any particular condition.
- Code assignment for 'with' vs 'without' abnormal findings depends on information known at the time of coding; if results are pending, 'without abnormal findings' is acceptable.
