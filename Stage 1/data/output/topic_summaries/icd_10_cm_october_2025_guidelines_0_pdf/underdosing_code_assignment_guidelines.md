# Underdosing Code Assignment Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Underdosing Code Assignment and SDOH Reporting

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign codes from categories T36-T50 with fifth or sixth character '6' for underdosing or self-discontinuation.
- Documentation of a change in the patient's condition is not required to assign an underdosing code.
- Underdosing codes should never be assigned as principal or first-listed codes.
- Use Noncompliance (Z91.12-, Z91.13-, Z91.14-, Z91.A4-) or complication of care (Y63.6-Y63.9) codes with underdosing codes to indicate intent.
- Assign Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) codes when social problems, conditions, or risk factors are documented in the medical record.
- Assign as many SDOH codes as necessary to describe all documented social problems, conditions, or risk factors during the current episode of care.
- SDOH code assignment may be based on documentation from clinicians other than the patient's provider (e.g., social workers, community health workers, case managers, nurses) if included in the official medical record.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Patient must be taking less medication than prescribed or have discontinued the prescribed medication on their own initiative.
- SDOH codes require documentation of a risk or unmet need for assistance, not just the status (e.g., living alone).
- SDOH documentation may come from clinicians other than the patient's provider (e.g., social workers, nurses) if included in the official medical record.
- Patient self-reported SDOH information must be signed off by and incorporated into the medical record by a clinician or provider.
- If a relapse or exacerbation occurs due to the dose reduction, the medical condition itself must be coded as the principal diagnosis.
- SDOH codes require documentation of a risk or unmet need for assistance; merely documenting a status (e.g., living alone) without such risk or need does not support code assignment.
- Patient self-reported SDOH information must be signed off by and incorporated into the medical record by a clinician or provider to be used for code assignment.
