# Principal Diagnosis Assignment for Malignancy
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Principal Diagnosis Assignment for Malignancy

**Key Behaviors:**
- Designate the primary malignancy as the principal/first-listed diagnosis if it is chiefly responsible for the admission/encounter and treatment is directed at the primary site.
- Assign the appropriate Z51.-code as the first-listed or principal diagnosis if the administration of chemotherapy, immunotherapy, or external beam radiation therapy is chiefly responsible for the admission/encounter.
- Sequence the primary site first, followed by any metastatic sites when the reason for the encounter is treatment of a primary malignancy.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- The malignancy must be chiefly responsible for occasioning the patient admission/encounter.
- Treatment must be directed at the primary site.
- The underlying diagnosis or problem for which the service is being performed must be assigned as a secondary diagnosis when a Z51.-code is the principal diagnosis.
