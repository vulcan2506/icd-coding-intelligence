# Y92 Place of Occurrence Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Y92 Place of Occurrence Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes from category Y92 are secondary codes used after other external cause codes to identify the location of the patient at the time of injury or other condition.
- A place of occurrence code is generally assigned only once, at the initial encounter for treatment.
- In rare instances where a new injury occurs during hospitalization, an additional place of occurrence code may be assigned.
- No 7th characters are used for Y92 codes.
- Do not use place of occurrence code Y92.9 if the place is not stated or is not applicable.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Y92 codes must be assigned as secondary codes following other external cause codes.
- Assignment is restricted to the initial encounter for treatment unless a new injury occurs during hospitalization.
- The place of occurrence must be stated and applicable; otherwise, Y92.9 is prohibited.
