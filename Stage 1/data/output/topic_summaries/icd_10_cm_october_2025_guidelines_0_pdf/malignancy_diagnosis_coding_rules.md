# Malignancy Diagnosis Coding Rules
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Malignancy Diagnosis Coding Rules

**Key Behaviors:**
- When the reason for admission/encounter is to determine the extent of the malignancy, the primary malignancy is designated as the principal diagnosis.
- When the reason for admission/encounter is for a procedure such as paracentesis or thoracentesis, the appropriate metastatic site is designated as the principal diagnosis.
- The primary malignancy or appropriate metastatic site is designated as the principal diagnosis even though chemotherapy or radiotherapy is administered.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The reason for admission or encounter must be to determine the extent of the malignancy.
- The reason for admission or encounter must be for a procedure such as paracentesis or thoracentesis.
- The primary malignancy or appropriate metastatic site must be designated as the principal or first-listed diagnosis.
