# Cerebrovascular Sequelae Coding (I69)
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Cerebrovascular Sequelae Coding (I69)

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes from category I69 are used to indicate conditions classifiable to categories I60-I67 as the causes of sequela (neurologic deficits).
- These 'late effects' include neurologic deficits that persist after initial onset of conditions classifiable to categories I60-I67.
- The neurologic deficits caused by cerebrovascular disease may be present from the onset or may arise at any time after the onset of the condition classifiable to categories I60-I67.
- Codes specifying hemiplegia, hemiparesis, and monoplegia identify whether the dominant or nondominant side is affected.
- If the affected side is documented but not specified as dominant or nondominant, default selection rules apply based on patient handedness and side affected.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- The condition must be classifiable to categories I60-I67 as the cause of the sequela.
- The neurologic deficits must persist after the initial onset of the cerebrovascular condition.
- For ambidextrous patients with unspecified dominant/nondominant side, the default should be dominant.
- If the left side is affected and the dominant/nondominant status is unspecified, the default is non-dominant.
- If the right side is affected and the dominant/nondominant status is unspecified, the default is dominant.
