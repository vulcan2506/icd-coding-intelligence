# Cerebrovascular Sequelae Coding (I69)
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Cerebrovascular Sequelae Coding (I69)

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes from category I69 are used to indicate conditions classifiable to categories I60-I67 as the causes of sequela (neurologic deficits) classified elsewhere.
- These 'late effects' include neurologic deficits that persist after the initial onset of conditions classifiable to categories I60-I67.
- If the affected side is documented but not specified as dominant or nondominant, default rules apply based on patient ambidexterity or side affected.
- For ambidextrous patients, the default side is dominant.
- If the left side is affected, the default is non-dominant; if the right side is affected, the default is dominant.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The condition must be a sequela (neurologic deficit) persisting after the initial onset of a cerebrovascular disease.
- The underlying cause must be classifiable to categories I60-I67.
- The specific neurologic deficit must be documented as a late effect rather than an acute onset.
- If side is unspecified, the coder must apply the default rules (ambidextrous=dominant, left=non-dominant, right=dominant) if no default is indicated in the classification system.
- The code must specify whether the dominant or nondominant side is affected when possible.
- The neurologic deficits may be present from the onset of the cerebrovascular disease or may arise at any time after the onset of the condition.
