# COVID-19 Principal Diagnosis Sequencing Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** COVID-19 Principal Diagnosis Sequencing

**Key Behaviors:**
- Code U07.1 (COVID-19) should be sequenced first when it meets the definition of principal diagnosis.
- Associated manifestations are sequenced after the principal COVID-19 code.
- Obstetrics, sepsis, and transplant complications take precedence over COVID-19 sequencing when applicable.
- Refer to specific sections for COVID-19 in pregnancy, newborns, and lung transplant patients.
- Follow external guidelines for sepsis, obstetrics, and transplant complications before applying general COVID-19 sequencing.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- COVID-19 must meet the definition of principal diagnosis to be sequenced first.
- Associated manifestations must be documented as part of the infection.
- Specific clinical scenarios (pregnancy, newborn, transplant) require adherence to their respective section guidelines.
- Sepsis and obstetric conditions must be identified to override standard sequencing.
- Transplant complications must be evaluated before assigning COVID-19 as the principal diagnosis.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Sequencing COVID-19 before obstetric, sepsis, or transplant complications when those conditions require primary sequencing.
- Assigning COVID-19 as principal diagnosis without verifying it meets the principal diagnosis definition.
- Ignoring specific section guidelines for pregnancy, newborns, or transplant patients.
- Coding associated manifestations before the principal COVID-19 code.
- Failing to reference external cause or specific condition sections for complex cases.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit sequencing rule for COVID-19 as principal diagnosis (U07.1).
- Mandatory reference to obstetrics, sepsis, and transplant complication guidelines.
- Specific guidance for coding COVID-19 in pregnancy, childbirth, and the puerperium.
- Dedicated section for COVID-19 infection in newborns.
- Updated protocol for lung transplant patients with COVID-19 infection.
