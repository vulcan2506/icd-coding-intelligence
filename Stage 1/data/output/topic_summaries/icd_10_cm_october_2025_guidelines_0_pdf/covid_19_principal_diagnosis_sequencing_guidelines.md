# COVID-19 Principal Diagnosis Sequencing Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** COVID-19 Principal Diagnosis Sequencing

**Key Behaviors:**
- Code U07.1 (COVID-19) should be sequenced first when it meets the definition of principal diagnosis.
- Associated manifestations are sequenced after the principal COVID-19 code.
- Exceptions exist for obstetrics, sepsis, and transplant complications which require specific sequencing rules.
- Refer to Section I.C.1.d for sepsis progression guidelines.
- Refer to Section I.C.15.s for pregnancy and childbirth guidelines.
- Associated manifestations of COVID-19 are sequenced after the principal U07.1 code.
- Specific sequencing rules for obstetrics, sepsis, and transplant complications override the general rule of sequencing U07.1 first.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- COVID-19 must meet the definition of principal diagnosis to be sequenced first.
- Other guidelines (e.g., obstetrics, sepsis, transplant) must be checked for specific sequencing requirements before applying the general rule.
- Other specific guidelines (obstetrics, sepsis, transplant) must be checked for sequencing requirements before applying the general rule.
