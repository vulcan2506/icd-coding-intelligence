# Pregnancy Complication Diagnosis Sequencing
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Pregnancy Complication Diagnosis Sequencing

**Key Behaviors:**
- In episodes without delivery, sequence the principal complication necessitating the encounter first.
- If multiple complications are treated or monitored, any may be sequenced first.
- When delivery occurs during admission, sequence the condition prompting admission as principal.
- If multiple conditions prompted admission, sequence the one most related to delivery first.
- Assign delivery complication codes as additional diagnoses, not principal.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Principal diagnosis must correspond to the complication necessitating the encounter if no delivery occurs.
- All treated or monitored complications must be considered for sequencing priority.
- Condition prompting admission takes precedence over delivery-related conditions when delivery occurs.
- For cesarean delivery, select the condition resulting in the procedure if it prompted admission.
- Select the admission reason condition as principal if unrelated to the condition resulting in cesarean delivery.
