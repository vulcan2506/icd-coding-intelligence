# Postoperative Pain Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Postoperative Pain Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- The provider's documentation should be used to guide the coding of postoperative pain.
- The default for post-thoracotomy and other postoperative pain not specified as acute or chronic is the code for the acute form.
- Routine or expected postoperative pain immediately after surgery should not be coded.
- Postoperative pain not associated with a specific postoperative complication is assigned to the appropriate postoperative pain code in category G89.
- Postoperative pain associated with a specific postoperative complication is assigned to the appropriate code(s) found in Chapter 19, Injury, poisoning, and certain other consequences of external causes.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Pain must be documented by the provider to be coded.
- Pain must not be routine or expected immediately after surgery.
- If pain is associated with a specific complication, the complication code from Chapter 19 must be used.
- If appropriate, additional codes from category G89 (G89.18 or G89.28) must be used to identify acute or chronic pain when associated with a complication.
- Coding must align with Section III and Section IV guidelines for reporting additional diagnoses and diagnostic coding in the outpatient setting.
