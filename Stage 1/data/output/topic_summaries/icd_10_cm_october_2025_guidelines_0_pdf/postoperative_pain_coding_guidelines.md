# Postoperative Pain Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Postoperative Pain Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- The provider's documentation should be used to guide the coding of postoperative pain.
- The default for post-thoracotomy and other postoperative pain not specified as acute or chronic is the code for the acute form.
- Routine or expected postoperative pain immediately after surgery should not be coded.
- Postoperative pain not associated with a specific postoperative complication is assigned to the appropriate postoperative pain code in category G89.
- Postoperative pain associated with a specific postoperative complication is assigned to the appropriate code(s) found in Chapter 19, Injury, poisoning, and certain other consequences of external causes.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Provider documentation must guide the coding of postoperative pain.
- Pain must be explicitly documented as acute or chronic to avoid defaulting to acute codes.
- Pain must not be routine or expected immediately after surgery to be coded.
- Pain must be associated with a specific postoperative complication to be coded in Chapter 19.
- Additional codes from category G89 (G89.18 or G89.28) must be used if appropriate to identify acute or chronic pain.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Coding routine or expected postoperative pain immediately after surgery.
- Defaulting to unspecified codes when acute or chronic status is not specified.
- Coding postoperative pain in Chapter 19 without an associated specific postoperative complication.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory use of provider documentation to determine coding for postoperative pain.
- Requirement to query for clarification if pain status (acute vs chronic) is unspecified.
- Specific instruction to use Chapter 19 codes for pain associated with specific complications like painful wire sutures.
