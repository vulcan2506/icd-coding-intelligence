# Secondary Diabetes Classification Codes
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Secondary Diabetes Classification Coding Standards

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes under categories E08, E09, and E13 identify complications or manifestations associated with secondary diabetes mellitus
- Secondary diabetes is always caused by another condition or event such as cystic fibrosis, malignant neoplasm of pancreas, pancreatectomy, adverse effect of drug, or poisoning
- Documentation must link the diabetes diagnosis to the specific underlying cause to justify the use of E08, E09, or E13 codes
- These categories distinguish secondary diabetes from primary diabetes types not caused by external conditions
- Coding requires explicit identification of the etiology (e.g., drug-induced, post-surgical, or disease-associated) to select the correct category

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Clinical documentation must specify the underlying condition or event causing the diabetes
- The diagnosis must be confirmed as secondary to the identified cause before assigning E08, E09, or E13 codes
