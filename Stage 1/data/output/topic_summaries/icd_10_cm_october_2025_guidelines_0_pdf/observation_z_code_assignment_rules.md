# Observation Z Code Assignment Rules
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Observation Z Code Assignment Rules

**Key Behaviors:**
- Observation Z codes are used only when a suspected condition is ruled out and no injury, illness, or signs/symptoms are present.
- Observation codes are primarily assigned as the principal/first-listed diagnosis.
- Codes from subcategory Z03.7 may be used as first-listed or additional codes for suspected maternal/fetal conditions ruled out during an encounter.
- For inconclusive suspected fetal conditions, assign codes from categories O35, O36, O40, or O41 instead of observation codes.
- Additional codes may be used with observation codes only if they are unrelated to the suspected condition being observed.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The suspected condition must be definitively ruled out during the encounter.
- No injury, illness, or signs/symptoms related to the suspected condition must be present.
- If the principal diagnosis is from category Z38, a code from category Z05 must be sequenced after it.
- Codes from subcategory Z03.7 must not be used for antenatal screening of mother.
- Confirmed conditions must be coded with the confirmed diagnosis code, not the observation code.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Using observation Z codes when an injury, illness, or signs/symptoms related to the suspected condition are present.
- Using observation Z codes for antenatal screening of mother.
- Using observation Z codes for suspected fetal conditions that are inconclusive following testing and evaluation.
- Assigning observation codes as secondary diagnoses when the condition is unrelated to the principal diagnosis without explicit observation context.
- Using observation codes when the condition is confirmed rather than ruled out.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit sequencing requirement for Z05 codes after Z38 codes for liveborn infants.
- Clarification that Z03.7 codes can serve as either first-listed or additional codes depending on the case.
- Mandatory use of O35, O36, O40, or O41 categories for inconclusive suspected fetal conditions.
- Restriction on using additional codes only when they are unrelated to the suspected condition being observed.
- Specific exclusion of Z03.7 codes for antenatal screening scenarios per Section I.C.21.
