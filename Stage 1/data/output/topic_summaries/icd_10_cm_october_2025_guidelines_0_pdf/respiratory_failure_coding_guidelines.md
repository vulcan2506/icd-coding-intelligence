# Respiratory Failure Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Respiratory Failure Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes in categories J44 and J45 distinguish between uncomplicated cases and those in acute exacerbation.
- An acute exacerbation is defined as a worsening or decompensation of a chronic condition.
- An acute exacerbation is not equivalent to an infection superimposed on a chronic condition, though an exacerbation may be triggered by an infection.
- Codes from subcategory J96.0 or J96.2 may be assigned as a principal diagnosis when the condition is chiefly responsible for the admission.
- Selection of principal diagnosis is supported by the Alphabetic Index and Tabular List, subject to chapter-specific sequencing precedence.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Condition must be established after study to be chiefly responsible for occasioning the admission to the hospital.
- Selection must be supported by the Alphabetic Index and Tabular List.
- Chapter-specific coding guidelines (e.g., obstetrics, poisoning, HIV, newborn) that provide sequencing direction take precedence over general guidelines.
