# Respiratory Failure Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Respiratory Failure Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes in categories J44 and J45 distinguish between uncomplicated cases and those in acute exacerbation.
- An acute exacerbation is defined as a worsening or decompensation of a chronic condition, not equivalent to an infection superimposed on a chronic condition.
- Codes from subcategory J96.0 or J96.2 may be assigned as a principal diagnosis when chiefly responsible for hospital admission.
- Selection of principal diagnosis must be supported by the Alphabetic Index and Tabular List.
- Chapter-specific coding guidelines (e.g., obstetrics, poisoning, HIV, newborn) take precedence over general respiratory failure sequencing.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The condition must be established after study to be chiefly responsible for occasioning the admission to the hospital.
- The selection must be supported by the Alphabetic Index and Tabular List.
- Chapter-specific guidelines must be checked for precedence before assigning principal diagnosis.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Coding an acute exacerbation as equivalent to an infection superimposed on a chronic condition.
