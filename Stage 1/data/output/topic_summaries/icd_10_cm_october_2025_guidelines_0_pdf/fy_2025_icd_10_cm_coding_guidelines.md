# FY 2025 ICD-10-CM Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** FY 2025 ICD-10-CM Coding Guidelines & Neoplasm Classification

**Key Behaviors:**
- Guidelines serve as companion rules to the official ICD-10-CM classification, with classification instructions taking precedence over guidelines.
- Adherence to guidelines is required under HIPAA for assigning diagnosis codes in all healthcare settings.
- A joint effort between the healthcare provider and the coder is essential for accurate documentation and code assignment.
- The entire medical record must be reviewed to determine the specific reason for the encounter and conditions treated.
- Primary malignant neoplasms overlapping two or more contiguous sites must be classified to the subcategory/code .8 ('overlapping lesion') unless indexed elsewhere.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Diagnosis codes must be assigned based on the specific reason for the encounter and conditions treated as documented in the entire medical record.
- The term 'provider' refers to a physician or qualified health care practitioner legally accountable for establishing the patient's diagnosis.
- Accurate coding cannot be achieved without consistent and complete documentation in the medical record.
- Neoplasm coding requires determining if the lesion is benign, in-situ, malignant, or of uncertain histologic behavior.
- For multiple non-contiguous neoplasms of the same site, codes for each distinct site must be assigned.
- The guidelines are organized into four specific sections: Section I for structure/conventions, Section II for principal diagnosis in non-outpatient settings, Section III for additional diagnoses in non-outpatient settings, and Section IV for outpatient coding.
- It is necessary to review all sections of the guidelines to fully understand the rules and instructions needed to code properly.

**New in this version:**
- Updated guidelines for FY 2026 (October 1, 2025 - September 30, 2026) include narrative changes in bold text and moved items underlined.
- Specific rule for primary malignant neoplasms overlapping contiguous sites to be classified as overlapping lesions (.8).
- Narrative changes in the FY 2026 guidelines appear in bold text.
- Items that have been moved within the guidelines since the April 2025 version are indicated by underlining.
- Italics are used to indicate revisions to heading changes.
