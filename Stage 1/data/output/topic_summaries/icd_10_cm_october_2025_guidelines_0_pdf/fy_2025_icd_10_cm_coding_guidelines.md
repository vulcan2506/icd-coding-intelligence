# FY 2025 ICD-10-CM Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** FY 2026 ICD-10-CM Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Guidelines are organized into sections covering structure, principal diagnosis selection, additional diagnoses, and outpatient coding.
- Instructions and conventions within the ICD-10-CM classification take precedence over general guidelines.
- Primary malignant neoplasms overlapping two or more contiguous sites should be classified to the subcategory/code .8 ('overlapping lesion').
- For multiple neoplasms of the same site that are not contiguous, codes for each site should be assigned.
- Adherence to these guidelines is required under HIPAA for assigning ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes.
- The term 'encounter' encompasses all healthcare settings, including hospital admissions.
- The term 'provider' refers specifically to a physician or any qualified health care practitioner legally accountable for establishing the patient's diagnosis.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- The entire medical record should be reviewed to determine the specific reason for the encounter and conditions treated.
- A joint effort between the healthcare provider and the coder is essential for accurate documentation and code assignment.
- Documentation must indicate if a neoplasm is benign, in-situ, malignant, or of uncertain histologic behavior.
- If a neoplasm is malignant, any secondary (metastatic) sites must also be determined.
- The combination of overlapping sites must not be specifically indexed elsewhere before using the .8 code.
- The guidelines are organized into four specific sections: Section I (structure/conventions), Section II (principal diagnosis for non-outpatient), Section III (additional diagnoses for non-outpatient), and Section IV (outpatient coding).
- Chapter 16 contains specific rules for conditions originating in the perinatal period (P00-P96), including observation/evaluation of newborns and coding additional perinatal diagnoses.
