# ICD-10-CM Burn and Corrosion Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Burn and Corrosion Coding Distinctions and Sequela Reporting

**Key Behaviors:**
- Distinguish between thermal/electric/radiation burns and chemical corrosions, applying the same guidelines to both.
- Classify burns by depth (first, second, third degree) and site, while classifying eye and internal organ burns by site only.
- Assign separate codes for each burn site unless documentation is unspecified, in which case use 'multiple sites' codes.
- Use extent-based categories (T31/T32) when site is unspecified or for additional data on mortality reporting (e.g., third-degree burns involving 20%+ BSA).
- Allow simultaneous assignment of current burn codes (7th char 'A' or 'D') and sequelae codes (7th char 'S') when both conditions exist.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Burns must be classified by depth (erythema, blistering, full-thickness) and extent using the 'rule of nines' adjusted for age/anatomy.
- Codes from categories T31 and T32 must not be used for sequelae of burns or corrosions.
- Category T30 (unspecified body region) should rarely be used due to vagueness.
- Percentage assignments for body surface area may be modified for infants and children to accommodate anatomical differences.
- Documentation must specify individual sites to avoid defaulting to 'multiple sites' codes.
- Codes from categories T31 and T32 should not be used for sequelae of burns or corrosions.
- Category T30 (Burn and corrosion, body region unspecified) should rarely be used due to vagueness.
