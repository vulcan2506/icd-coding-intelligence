# ICD-10-CM Burn and Corrosion Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Burn and Corrosion Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Distinguish between thermal/electric/radiation burns and chemical corrosions, applying the same guidelines to both.
- Classify burns by depth (first, second, third degree) and site, while classifying eye and internal organ burns by site only.
- Assign separate codes for each burn site unless documentation is unspecified, in which case use 'multiple sites' codes.
- Use extent-based categories (T31/T32) when the specific site is not specified or for reporting third-degree burns involving 20%+ of body surface.
- Apply the 'rule of nines' for body surface estimation, adjusting percentages for infants, children, and patients with atypical proportions.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must specify the depth, extent, and agent of the burn or corrosion for accurate coding.
- Category T30 (unspecified body region) should rarely be used due to its vagueness.
- Codes from categories T31 and T32 must not be used for sequelae of burns or corrosions.
- When both a current burn and sequelae of an old burn exist, assign both the current code (7th char 'A' or 'D') and the sequelae code (7th char 'S').
- Providers may adjust 'rule of nines' percentage assignments to accommodate specific patient anatomical variations.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Using Category T30 as a primary code for unspecified body regions.
- Assigning extent-based codes (T31/T32) for sequelae of burns or corrosions.
- Failing to adjust 'rule of nines' percentages for pediatric or anatomically unique patients.
- Coding a single code for burns at multiple distinct sites without specifying individual sites.
- Omitting the 7th character extension when documenting both current and sequela conditions.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit distinction between thermal/electric/radiation burns and chemical corrosions in the guideline text.
- Mandatory use of 7th character extensions ('A', 'D', 'S') to differentiate current, healing, and sequela states.
- Advisory to use T31/T32 for evaluating burn mortality and reporting third-degree burns involving 20%+ of body surface.
- Clarification that eye and internal organ burns (T26-T28) are classified by site, not degree.
- Guidance on modifying 'rule of nines' for infants, children, and patients with large buttocks, thighs, or abdomen.
