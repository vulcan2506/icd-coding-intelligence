# Pregnancy and Puerperium Z-Code Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Pregnancy and Puerperium Z-Code Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Z codes for pregnancy are used when no problems or complications from the Obstetrics chapter exist (e.g., routine prenatal or postpartum care).
- Codes in category Z34 (Encounter for supervision of normal pregnancy) are always first listed and must not be used with any other code from the OB chapter.
- Codes in category Z3A (Weeks of gestation) provide additional pregnancy information but are excluded for abortive outcomes, elective terminations, and postpartum conditions.
- The outcome of delivery (category Z37) must be included on all maternal delivery records as a secondary code.
- Family planning or procreative management Z codes should be included on obstetric records during pregnancy or the postpartum stage if applicable.
- Codes in category Z3A may be assigned to provide additional information about the pregnancy.
- Z codes for family planning (contraceptive) or procreative management and counseling should be included on an obstetric record either during the pregnancy or the postpartum stage, if applicable.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Z34 codes must be the first listed code and cannot be combined with other OB chapter codes.
- Z3A codes must not be assigned for pregnancies with abortive outcomes (categories O00-O08), elective termination (Z33.2), or postpartum conditions.
- The date of admission must be used to determine weeks of gestation for inpatient admissions spanning multiple gestational weeks.
- Category Z37 codes must be secondary and should not be used on newborn records.
- Family planning or procreative management Z codes are only included if applicable to the specific pregnancy or postpartum stage.
- The date of the admission should be used to determine weeks of gestation for inpatient admissions that encompass more than one gestational week.
