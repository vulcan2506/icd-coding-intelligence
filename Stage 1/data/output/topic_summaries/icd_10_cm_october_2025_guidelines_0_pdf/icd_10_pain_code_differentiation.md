# ICD-10 Pain Code Differentiation
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10 Pain Code Differentiation

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes G89.0 and G89.4 should only be used when the provider has specifically documented Central pain syndrome or Chronic pain syndrome, as these are distinct from the general term 'chronic pain'.
- Pain associated with devices, implants, or grafts left in a surgical site is assigned to appropriate codes in Chapter 19 (Injury, poisoning, and certain other consequences of external causes).
- Specific codes for pain due to medical devices are found in the T code section of ICD-10-CM.
- Use additional code(s) from category G89 to identify acute or chronic pain due to the presence of a device, implant, or graft (G89.18 or G89.28).
- Refer to Section I.C.5 for guidelines on Pain disorders related to psychological factors.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Provider must specifically document 'Central pain syndrome' to assign code G89.0.
- Provider must specifically document 'Chronic pain syndrome' to assign code G89.4.
- Documentation must distinguish between general 'chronic pain' and specific syndromes to avoid misclassification.
- Pain related to retained surgical devices must be coded using Chapter 19 codes combined with G89.18 or G89.28.
- Coding for device-related pain requires the use of T codes from the ICD-10-CM T section.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Assigning G89.0 or G89.4 based solely on the term 'chronic pain' without specific syndrome documentation.
- Coding pain due to retained implants solely with G89 codes without referencing Chapter 19 or T codes.
- Using unspecified pain codes for device-related pain instead of the specific G89.18/G89.28 combination.
- Ignoring the distinction between psychological factors and physical device-related pain in coding assignments.
- Failing to query providers when documentation is ambiguous between general pain and specific syndromes.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory use of G89.18 or G89.28 as additional codes for acute or chronic pain due to devices, implants, or grafts.
- Requirement to cross-reference Chapter 19 codes for pain associated with retained surgical items.
- Explicit instruction to consult Section I.C.5 for pain disorders related to psychological factors.
- Clarification that 'chronic pain' is not synonymous with 'chronic pain syndrome' for coding purposes.
- Updated guidance on differentiating central pain syndrome (G89.0) from other pain conditions.
