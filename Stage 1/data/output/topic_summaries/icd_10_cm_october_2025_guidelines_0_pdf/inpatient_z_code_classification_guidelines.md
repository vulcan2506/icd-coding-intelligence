# Inpatient Z-Code Classification Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Inpatient Z-Code Classification and Encounter Logic

**Key Behaviors:**
- Z codes may only be reported as the principal/first-listed diagnosis unless multiple encounters on the same day have combined medical records.
- Specific Z code exceptions apply, such as Z00.6 being excluded from the general examination rule.
- Infertility and pregnancy-related Z codes (e.g., Z31.81) have specific mapping rules for assisted reproductive procedures, fertility preservation, and pregnancy supervision.
- Administrative examinations (Z02) and examinations for other reasons (Z04) follow standard principal diagnosis reporting rules.
- Elective termination of pregnancy (Z33.2) is restricted to principal diagnosis reporting unless combined with other encounters.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Medical records for multiple encounters on the same day must be combined to justify reporting a Z code as a non-principal diagnosis.
- Documentation must explicitly support the specific Z code category (e.g., general examination, administrative, or special examination) without a complaint or suspected diagnosis.
- For infertility encounters, the specific procedure type (assisted reproductive, preservation, or supervision) must be documented to select the correct mapped Z code.
- Z00.6 must be excluded from the general rule allowing Z codes as principal diagnoses for general examinations.
- Elective termination of pregnancy (Z33.2) requires clear documentation of the elective nature to be reported as the principal diagnosis.
- Z00.6 is explicitly excluded from the list of Z codes that may be reported as the principal diagnosis for general examinations.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Reporting Z codes as secondary diagnoses when multiple encounters on the same day are not combined into a single medical record.
- Using unspecified Z codes for infertility or pregnancy supervision without mapping to the specific procedure codes defined in the guidelines.
- Assigning Z00.6 as a principal diagnosis under the general examination rule without verifying specific exclusion criteria.
- Failing to map male factor infertility in female patients to the correct assisted reproductive or fertility preservation Z codes.
- Reporting Z33.2 as a secondary diagnosis without evidence of combined medical records for multiple encounters.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit mapping rules for Z31.81 to Z31.83, Z31.84, and Z34 based on the specific clinical context of infertility and pregnancy.
- Clarification that Z codes can be reported as non-principal diagnoses only when medical records for multiple same-day encounters are combined.
- Inclusion of Z04 for examinations and observation for other reasons as a distinct category from general or special examinations.
- Updated guidance on handling elective termination of pregnancy (Z33.2) as a principal diagnosis with specific exceptions.
- Mandatory exclusion of Z00.6 from the general rule allowing Z codes as principal diagnoses for general examinations.
- A specific mapping table defines the relationship between 'Encounter for male factor infertility in female patient' (Z31.81) and specific pregnancy/outcome codes (Z31.83, Z31.84, Z34) based on the clinical context of assisted reproductive procedures, fertility preservation, or pregnancy supervision.
