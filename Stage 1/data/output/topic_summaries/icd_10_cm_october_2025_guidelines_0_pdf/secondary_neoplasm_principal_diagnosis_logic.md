# Secondary Neoplasm Principal Diagnosis Logic
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Secondary Neoplasm Principal Diagnosis Logic

**Key Behaviors:**
- Designate the secondary neoplasm as the principal diagnosis when treatment is directed toward the secondary site only.
- Apply this logic even if the primary malignancy remains present in the patient.
- Prioritize the secondary site over the primary site for principal diagnosis assignment based on treatment focus.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must confirm the patient was admitted because of the primary neoplasm with metastasis.
- Clinical records must explicitly state that treatment is directed toward the secondary site only.
- The presence of the primary malignancy must be acknowledged but not the focus of the current treatment plan.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Assigning the primary neoplasm as the principal diagnosis when the secondary site is the sole focus of treatment.
- Ignoring the treatment direction when determining the principal diagnosis for metastatic cases.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit rule allowing secondary neoplasm to supersede primary neoplasm for principal diagnosis based on treatment intent.
