# ICD-10 History Z Code Implementation
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10 History Z Code Implementation

**Key Behaviors:**
- Personal history codes explain past medical conditions no longer present but with potential for recurrence requiring monitoring.
- Family history codes are used when a patient has a family member with a disease causing higher risk of contracting it.
- History codes are acceptable on any medical record regardless of the reason for visit.
- The reason for the encounter (e.g., screening or counseling) should be sequenced first, with history codes assigned as additional diagnoses.
- History codes may be used in conjunction with follow-up or screening codes to explain the need for a test or procedure.
- Personal history codes apply to conditions that no longer exist and are not receiving any treatment.
- Family history codes apply when a family member has had a particular disease causing higher risk.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Condition must be documented as a past medical history for personal history codes.
- Risk must be established via family member disease for family history codes.
- Reason for encounter must be sequenced before history codes.
- History codes must be assigned as additional diagnoses, not primary, when a reason for encounter exists.
- Codes must reflect that the condition is no longer present (personal) or represents risk (family).
- Personal history codes require the condition to be documented as no longer existing and not receiving treatment.
- Family history codes require documentation that a family member has had a particular disease.
