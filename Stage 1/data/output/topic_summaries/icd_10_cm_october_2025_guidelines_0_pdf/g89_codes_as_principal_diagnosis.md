# G89 Codes as Principal Diagnosis
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** G89 Codes as Principal Diagnosis

**Key Behaviors:**
- Category G89 codes are acceptable as principal diagnosis when pain control is the reason for admission.
- The underlying cause of the pain must be reported as an additional diagnosis if known.
- Assign the appropriate pain code as principal when admitted for neurostimulator insertion for pain control.
- Assign the underlying condition code as principal if the admission is for treating the condition and a neurostimulator is inserted for pain control during the same encounter.
- Assign the pain code as a secondary diagnosis when the underlying condition is the principal diagnosis.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Pain control or pain management must be the documented reason for the admission or encounter.
- The underlying cause of the pain must be known and documented to be reported as an additional diagnosis.
- The procedure must be aimed at treating the underlying condition for the underlying code to take precedence over the pain code.
