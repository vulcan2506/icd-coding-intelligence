# Chapter 15 Pregnancy Complication Coding
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Chapter 15 Pregnancy Complication Coding

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assess if a condition was pre-existing prior to pregnancy or developed during/due to pregnancy to assign the correct code.
- Categories not distinguishing between pre-existing and pregnancy-related conditions may be used for either.
- Codes specifically for the puerperium may be used with codes complicating pregnancy and childbirth if a condition arises postpartum during the delivery encounter.
- Chapter 15 codes may be used to describe pregnancy-related complications after the peripartum or postpartum period if the provider documents that a condition is pregnancy related.
- Codes from Chapter 15 may be used as additional codes to identify documented complications of the pregnancy in conjunction with codes in categories O04, O07, and O08.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Provider must document that a condition is pregnancy related to use Chapter 15 codes after the peripartum or postpartum period.
- No complications should be noted when assigning Z39.0 for admission for routine postpartum care following delivery outside the hospital.
- Codes from Chapter 15 should be assigned as additional codes when identifying documented complications of the pregnancy.
- When assigning codes from Chapter 15, it is important to assess if a condition was pre-existing prior to pregnancy or developed during or due to the pregnancy in order to assign the correct code.
