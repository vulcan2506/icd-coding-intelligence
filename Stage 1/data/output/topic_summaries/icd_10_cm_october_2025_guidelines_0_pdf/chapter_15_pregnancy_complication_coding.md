# Chapter 15 Pregnancy Complication Coding
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Chapter 15 Pregnancy Complication Coding

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assess if a condition was pre-existing prior to pregnancy or developed during/due to pregnancy to assign the correct code.
- Use codes specifically for the puerperium with codes complicating pregnancy and childbirth if a condition arises postpartum during the delivery encounter.
- Use Chapter 15 codes to describe pregnancy-related complications after the peripartum or postpartum period if the provider documents that a condition is pregnancy related.
- Assign code Z39.0 as the principal diagnosis when the mother delivers outside the hospital and is admitted for routine postpartum care with no complications.
- Use Chapter 15 codes as additional codes to identify documented complications of the pregnancy in conjunction with codes in categories O04, O07, and O08.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Provider must document that a condition is pregnancy related to use Chapter 15 codes after the peripartum or postpartum period.
- No complications must be noted to assign Z39.0 as the principal diagnosis for routine postpartum care following outside hospital delivery.
- Conditions must be distinguished between pre-existing and pregnancy-related to ensure correct code assignment.
