# Outpatient ICD-10-CM Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Outpatient ICD-10-CM Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Guidelines in Section I (Conventions, general, and chapter-specific) must be applied to outpatient services and office visits.
- The terms encounter and visit are used interchangeably to describe outpatient service contacts.
- Coding guidelines for outpatient and provider reporting of diagnoses vary from those for inpatient diagnoses.
- The Uniform Hospital Discharge Data Set (UHDDS) definition of principal diagnosis does not apply to hospital-based outpatient services and provider-based office visits.
- Coding guidelines for inconclusive diagnoses (probable, suspected, rule out, etc.) do not apply to outpatients.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Section IA conventions must be referenced for abbreviations, punctuation, and symbols.
- Section I.B general guidelines must be applied to the entire classification.
- Section I.C chapter-specific guidelines must correspond to the chapters in the classification.
- Correct code sequence procedures described in Section I must be followed.
- Diagnoses must not be coded as inconclusive (probable, suspected, rule out) for outpatient settings.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Using the UHDDS definition of principal diagnosis for hospital-based outpatient services.
- Applying inpatient coding guidelines for inconclusive diagnoses (probable, suspected, rule out) to outpatients.
- Distinguishing between 'encounter' and 'visit' terms in outpatient guideline descriptions.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit approval of guidelines for use by hospitals/providers in coding and reporting hospital-based outpatient services.
- Explicit approval of guidelines for use by hospitals/providers in coding and reporting provider-based office visits.
