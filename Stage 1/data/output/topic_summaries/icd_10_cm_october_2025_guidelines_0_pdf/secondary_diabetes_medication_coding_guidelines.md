# Secondary Diabetes Medication Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Secondary Diabetes Medication Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign additional codes from category Z79 for patients with secondary diabetes mellitus who routinely use insulin, oral hypoglycemic drugs, or injectable non-insulin drugs.
- Assign both Z79.4 and Z79.84 when a patient is treated with both oral hypoglycemic drugs and insulin.
- Assign Z79.4 and Z79.85 when a patient is treated with both insulin and an injectable non-insulin antidiabetic drug.
- Assign Z79.84 and Z79.85 when a patient is treated with both oral hypoglycemic drugs and an injectable non-insulin antidiabetic drug.
- Codes must identify the long-term (current) use of the specified medications.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Patient must have secondary diabetes mellitus.
- Patient must routinely use the specified medication (insulin, oral hypoglycemic drugs, or injectable non-insulin drugs).
- Documentation must confirm long-term (current) use of the medication.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory assignment of Z79 codes for secondary diabetes patients on long-term medication regimens.
- Specific code pairing rules for combinations of oral, injectable, and insulin therapies.
