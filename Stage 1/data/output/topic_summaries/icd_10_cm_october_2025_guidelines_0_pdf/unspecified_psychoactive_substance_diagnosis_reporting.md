# Unspecified Psychoactive Substance Diagnosis Reporting
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Unspecified Psychoactive Substance Diagnosis Reporting

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes for unspecified psychoactive substance use (F10.9- through F19.9-) should only be assigned based on provider documentation.
- These codes must meet the definition of a reportable diagnosis as per Section III guidelines.
- Codes are to be used only when the substance use is associated with a substance-related disorder or medical condition.
- The relationship between the substance use and the associated disorder/condition must be explicitly documented by the provider.
- Assignment requires verification that the unspecified code is not used in isolation without a documented associated condition.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Provider documentation must explicitly link the unspecified psychoactive substance use to a specific disorder or medical condition.
- The diagnosis must satisfy the criteria for being a reportable additional diagnosis.
- Documentation must confirm the association is not incidental but clinically relevant.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Assigning unspecified psychoactive substance codes without a documented associated disorder or medical condition.
- Using these codes when the substance use is not linked to a specific clinical outcome or disorder.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory requirement to document the specific relationship between the unspecified substance use and the associated disorder/condition.
