# Sign and Symptom Coding Guidelines
*Source: icd_10_cm_october_2025_guidelines_0.pdf*

**Feature:** Sign and Symptom Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Report codes for signs/symptoms or unspecified codes when a definitive diagnosis has not been established by the end of the encounter.
- Use unspecified codes when sufficient clinical information is not available to assign a more specific code, provided they accurately reflect known conditions.
- Report sign/symptom codes in addition to a related definitive diagnosis only when the sign or symptom is not routinely associated with that diagnosis.
- Sequence the definitive diagnosis code before the symptom code when both are reported.
- Do not assign codes for signs or symptoms that are routinely associated with a disease process as additional codes unless otherwise instructed.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Definitive diagnosis must be supported by available medical record documentation and clinical knowledge before assigning specific diagnosis codes.
- Coding must reflect the level of certainty known for the specific healthcare encounter.
- Sign/symptom codes may be reported alongside a definitive diagnosis only if the symptom is not routinely associated with the diagnosis.
- Specific codes must be supported by medical record documentation; medically unnecessary diagnostic testing to determine specificity is inappropriate.
- Joint effort between healthcare provider and coder is essential to achieve complete documentation and accurate code assignment.
