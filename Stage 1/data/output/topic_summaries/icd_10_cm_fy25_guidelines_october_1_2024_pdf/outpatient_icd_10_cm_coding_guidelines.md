# Outpatient ICD-10-CM Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Outpatient ICD-10-CM Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Guidelines in Section I (Conventions, general, and chapter-specific) must be applied to outpatient services and office visits.
- The terms encounter and visit are used interchangeably to describe outpatient service contacts.
- Coding guidelines for outpatient and provider reporting vary from inpatient diagnoses in specific instances.
- The Uniform Hospital Discharge Data Set (UHDDS) definition of principal diagnosis does not apply to hospital-based outpatient services or provider-based office visits.
- Coding guidelines for inconclusive diagnoses (probable, suspected, rule out, etc.) do not apply to outpatients.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Section IA conventions must be referenced for abbreviations, punctuation, and symbols used in the Tabular List.
- Section I.B general guidelines must be applied to the entire classification for outpatient services.
- Section I.C chapter-specific guidelines must correspond to the chapters arranged in the classification.
- Correct code sequence determination must follow the descriptions provided in Section I.
- Diagnosis coding must exclude inpatient-specific definitions like UHDDS principal diagnosis for outpatient settings.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Application of the UHDDS definition of principal diagnosis to hospital-based outpatient services.
- Application of coding guidelines for inconclusive diagnoses (probable, suspected, rule out) to outpatient reporting.
- Distinguishing between 'encounter' and 'visit' terms in the context of outpatient service descriptions.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit approval of these guidelines for use by hospitals and providers in coding and reporting hospital-based outpatient services.
- Explicit approval of these guidelines for use by hospitals and providers in coding and reporting provider-based office visits.
