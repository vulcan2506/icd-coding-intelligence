# Underdosing Code Assignment Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Underdosing Code Assignment and SDOH Integration

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign codes from categories T36-T50 with fifth or sixth character '6' for underdosing or self-discontinuation.
- Never assign underdosing codes as principal or first-listed codes; code the underlying medical condition if present.
- Use Noncompliance (Z91.12-, Z91.13-, Z91.14-, Z91.A4-) or complication of care (Y63.6-Y63.9) codes with underdosing codes to indicate intent.
- Assign Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) codes based on documented social problems, conditions, or risk factors influencing health.
- Allow patient self-reported SDOH information if signed off and incorporated into the medical record by a clinician or provider.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must state the patient is taking less medication than prescribed or discontinued the medication on their own initiative.
- Documentation of a change in the patient's condition is not required to assign an underdosing code.
- SDOH codes require documentation of specific social problems, conditions, or risk factors, not just the presence of a social circumstance.
- SDOH documentation may come from clinicians other than the patient's provider (e.g., social workers, case managers) if included in the official medical record.
- Patient self-reported SDOH data must be verified and signed off by a clinician or provider to be valid for coding.
- Assign as many SDOH codes as are necessary to describe all documented social problems, conditions, or risk factors during the current episode of care.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Assigning underdosing codes as principal or first-listed diagnoses.
- Requiring documentation of a change in patient condition to validate an underdosing code.
- Assigning SDOH codes based solely on the existence of a social circumstance without documented risk or unmet need.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit inclusion of Noncompliance (Z91.12-, Z91.13-, Z91.14-, Z91.A4-) and complication of care (Y63.6-Y63.9) codes to indicate intent alongside underdosing codes.
- Expanded authority to use SDOH documentation from non-provider clinicians (social workers, community health workers, case managers, nurses) for code assignment.
- Validation of patient self-reported SDOH information through clinician or provider sign-off and incorporation into the medical record.
- SDOH codes for categories Z55-Z65 may be assigned based on documentation from clinicians other than the patient's provider (e.g., social workers, community health workers, case managers, nurses) if included in the official medical record.
