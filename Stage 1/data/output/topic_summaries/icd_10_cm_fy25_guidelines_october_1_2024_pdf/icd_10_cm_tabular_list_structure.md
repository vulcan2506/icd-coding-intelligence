# ICD-10-CM Tabular List Structure
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10-CM Tabular List Structure

**Key Behaviors:**
- Categories are strictly 3 characters and serve as the highest level of subdivision.
- Subcategories are either 4 or 5 characters long.
- Final codes may be 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7 characters depending on the condition.
- Codes with applicable 7th characters are still referred to as codes, not subcategories.
- A code containing an applicable 7th character is considered invalid without that character.
- Characters for categories, subcategories, and codes may be either a letter or a number.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- All categories must be exactly 3 characters.
- Subcategories must be exactly 4 or 5 characters.
- Codes with applicable 7th characters must include the 7th character to be valid.
- The final level of subdivision in the hierarchy is designated as a code.
- The list uses an indented format to denote hierarchical relationships.
