# Observation Z Code Assignment Rules
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Observation Z Code Assignment Rules

**Key Behaviors:**
- Observation Z codes are for use only when a suspected condition is ruled out and no injury, illness, or signs/symptoms are present.
- Observation codes are primarily used as a principal/first-listed diagnosis unless the patient is observed for a ruled-out condition unrelated to the principal diagnosis.
- Codes from subcategory Z03.7 may be used as first-listed or additional codes for suspected maternal/fetal conditions ruled out during an encounter.
- For inconclusive suspected fetal conditions following testing, assign codes from categories O35, O36, O40, or O41 instead of observation codes.
- Codes from subcategory Z03.7 must not be used for antenatal screening of the mother.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The suspected condition must be definitively ruled out during the encounter.
- No injury, illness, or signs/symptoms related to the suspected condition may be present.
- Additional codes may only be assigned if they are unrelated to the suspected condition being observed.
- If the principal diagnosis is from category Z38, a code from category Z05 must be sequenced after it.
- Confirmed conditions must be coded with their specific diagnosis/symptom codes rather than observation codes.
- Codes from subcategory Z03.7 must not be used for encounters for antenatal screening of the mother.
- For encounters for suspected fetal conditions that are inconclusive following testing and evaluation, assign codes from categories O35, O36, O40, or O41 instead of observation codes.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Using observation Z codes when an injury, illness, or signs/symptoms related to the suspected condition are present.
- Using observation Z codes for antenatal screening of the mother.
- Using observation Z codes for suspected fetal conditions that are inconclusive following testing and evaluation.
- Assigning observation codes as secondary diagnoses when the observed condition is related to the principal diagnosis without explicit guidance.
- Using observation codes for confirmed conditions instead of the appropriate confirmed diagnosis codes.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit sequencing rule requiring Z05 codes to follow Z38 codes when the principal diagnosis is a liveborn infant.
- Clarification that Z03.7 codes can serve as either first-listed or additional codes depending on the specific case context.
- Mandatory use of categories O35, O36, O40, or O41 for inconclusive suspected fetal conditions to replace observation codes.
- Restriction on using Z04.9 for unspecified reasons while allowing other Z04 codes for specific examination and observation reasons.
- Requirement to query or document the ruling out of the suspected condition before assigning any observation Z code.
- Explicit restriction that Z04.9 (Encounter for examination and observation for unspecified reason) is excluded from the Z04 subcategory, while other Z04 codes remain valid for specific examination and observation reasons.
