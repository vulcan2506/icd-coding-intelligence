# Observation Z Code Assignment Rules
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Observation Z Code Assignment Rules

**Key Behaviors:**
- Observation Z codes are used in very limited circumstances when a suspected condition is ruled out.
- Observation codes are not used if an injury, illness, or signs/symptoms related to the suspected condition are present.
- Observation codes are primarily used as a principal/first-listed diagnosis.
- Observation codes may be assigned as secondary diagnoses when unrelated to the principal diagnosis.
- Codes from subcategory Z03.7 may be used as first-listed or additional codes for suspected maternal/fetal conditions ruled out during an encounter.
- Codes from subcategory Z03.7 may be used as a first-listed diagnosis depending on the case.
- When the principal diagnosis must be from category Z38, a code from category Z05 is sequenced immediately after the Z38 code.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- The suspected condition must be ruled out during the encounter.
- No injury, illness, or signs/symptoms related to the suspected condition must be present.
- Additional codes may only be used if they are unrelated to the suspected condition being observed.
- For suspected fetal conditions that are inconclusive, assign codes from categories O35, O36, O40, or O41 instead of Z codes.
- Codes from subcategory Z03.7 are not for use in encounters for antenatal screening of mother.
- Additional codes may only be used in addition to observation codes if they are unrelated to the suspected condition being observed or evaluated.
