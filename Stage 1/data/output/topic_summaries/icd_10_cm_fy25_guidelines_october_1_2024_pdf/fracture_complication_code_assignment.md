# Fracture Complication Code Assignment
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Fracture Complication Code Assignment

**Key Behaviors:**
- 7th character A is assigned when the patient is receiving active treatment for the fracture, regardless of provider continuity.
- 7th character D is assigned for encounters after active treatment completion during routine healing or recovery care.
- Other 7th characters are used for subsequent encounters treating problems associated with healing, such as malunions, nonunions, and sequelae.
- Complications of surgical treatment for fracture repairs during the healing or recovery phase must be coded with appropriate complication codes.
- Assignment of the 7th character is based on active treatment status rather than whether the provider is seeing the patient for the first time.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must confirm the patient is undergoing active treatment to justify 7th character A.
- Documentation must confirm active treatment has completed to justify 7th character D.
- Specific problems associated with healing (e.g., malunion, nonunion) must be documented to select appropriate subsequent encounter codes.
- Surgical complications must be explicitly identified as occurring during the healing or recovery phase.
- Coding must adhere to Section I.C.19 guidelines for traumatic fractures.
