# COVID-19 Diagnosis Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** COVID-19 Diagnosis Coding for Symptoms and Asymptomatic Cases

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign codes for presenting signs and symptoms (e.g., cough, shortness of breath, fever) when a definitive COVID-19 diagnosis is not established.
- Assign Z20.822 as an additional code for patients with signs/symptoms who have actual or suspected contact with or exposure to COVID-19.
- Code asymptomatic individuals who test positive for COVID-19 as having the infection, even in the absence of symptoms.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Definitive diagnosis must be established to assign a specific COVID-19 code; otherwise, code the presenting signs and symptoms.
- Documentation must confirm actual or suspected contact/exposure to justify the assignment of Z20.822 alongside symptom codes.
- Positive testing results are required to classify asymptomatic individuals as having the COVID-19 infection.

**New in this version:**
- Assignment of Z20.822 for contact with or suspected exposure to COVID-19 in patients presenting with symptoms but without confirmed diagnosis.
- Classification of asymptomatic individuals with positive test results as having the COVID-19 infection.
