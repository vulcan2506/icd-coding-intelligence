# ICD-10 History Z Code Implementation
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10 History Z Code Implementation

**Key Behaviors:**
- Personal history codes explain past medical conditions no longer present but with potential for recurrence requiring monitoring.
- Family history codes are used when a patient has a family member with a disease causing higher risk of contracting it.
- History codes are acceptable on any medical record regardless of the reason for visit.
- The reason for the encounter (e.g., screening) should be sequenced first, with history codes assigned as additional diagnoses.
- History codes may be used in conjunction with follow-up or screening codes to explain the need for a test or procedure.
- Personal history codes are used when a past medical condition no longer exists and is not receiving any treatment.
- Family history codes are used when a family member has had a particular disease causing higher risk for the patient.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Condition must be documented as a past medical history for personal history codes.
- Risk must be linked to a family member's disease for family history codes.
- Encounter reason (e.g., screening) must be sequenced before history codes.
- Condition must not be currently receiving treatment for personal history codes.
- History codes must be assigned as additional diagnoses, not primary, when a specific encounter reason exists.
- The past medical condition must no longer exist and not be receiving treatment for personal history codes to be valid.
- The family member must have had the disease (past tense) for family history codes to be applicable.
