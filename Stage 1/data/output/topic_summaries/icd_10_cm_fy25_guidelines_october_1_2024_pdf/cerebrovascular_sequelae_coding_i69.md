# Cerebrovascular Sequelae Coding (I69)
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Cerebrovascular Sequelae Coding (I69)

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes I69 indicate conditions classifiable to I60-I67 as causes of sequela (neurologic deficits) classified elsewhere.
- Codes specify whether the dominant or nondominant side is affected for hemiplegia, hemiparesis, and monoplegia.
- Default side assignment rules apply if affected side is documented but not specified as dominant or nondominant.
- Codes I69 may be assigned with codes I60-I67 if the patient has current cerebrovascular disease and deficits from an old cerebrovascular disease.
- Codes I69 should not be assigned if the patient does not have neurologic deficits.
- For ambidextrous patients where the affected side is unspecified, the default assignment is the dominant side.
- If the left side is affected but not specified as dominant or nondominant, the default assignment is non-dominant.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Neurologic deficits must be present from the onset or arise at any time after the onset of the condition classifiable to categories I60-I67.
- Affected side must be documented as dominant or nondominant; if not specified, defaults apply based on patient ambidexterity or side affected.
- Patient must have a current cerebrovascular disease and deficits from an old cerebrovascular disease to assign I69 with I60-I67.
- Patient must have neurologic deficits to assign codes from category I69.
- Personal history codes (Z86.73) may be used in conjunction with I69 codes per Section I.C.21.4.
