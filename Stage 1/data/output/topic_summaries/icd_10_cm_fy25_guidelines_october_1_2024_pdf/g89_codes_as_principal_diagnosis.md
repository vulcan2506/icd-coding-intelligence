# G89 Codes as Principal Diagnosis
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** G89 Codes as Principal Diagnosis

**Key Behaviors:**
- Category G89 codes are acceptable as principal or first-listed diagnosis when pain control or pain management is the reason for the admission/encounter.
- When a patient is admitted for the insertion of a neurostimulator for pain control, assign the appropriate pain code as the principal or first-listed diagnosis.
- When an admission is for a procedure aimed at treating the underlying condition and a neurostimulator is inserted for pain control, assign the code for the underlying condition as the principal diagnosis and the pain code as a secondary diagnosis.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- The underlying cause of the pain should be reported as an additional diagnosis if known when pain control is the reason for admission.
- The procedure must be aimed at treating the underlying condition for the underlying condition to be assigned as the principal diagnosis in neurostimulator cases.
