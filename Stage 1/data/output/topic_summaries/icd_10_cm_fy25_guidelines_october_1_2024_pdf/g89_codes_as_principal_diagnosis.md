# G89 Codes as Principal Diagnosis
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** G89 Codes as Principal Diagnosis

**Key Behaviors:**
- Category G89 codes are acceptable as principal or first-listed diagnosis when pain control or pain management is the reason for the admission/encounter.
- When a patient is admitted for the insertion of a neurostimulator for pain control, assign the appropriate pain code as the principal or first-listed diagnosis.
- If an admission is for a procedure aimed at treating the underlying condition and a neurostimulator is inserted for pain control during the same encounter, assign the code for the underlying condition as the principal diagnosis and the pain code as a secondary diagnosis.
- The underlying cause of the pain should be reported as an additional diagnosis if known when pain control is the reason for admission.
- G89 codes can serve as the first-listed code in encounters where the primary focus is pain management rather than the etiology of the pain.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Pain control or pain management must be explicitly documented as the reason for the admission or encounter.
- The underlying cause of the pain must be identified and reported as an additional diagnosis if known.
- Clinical documentation must distinguish between admissions for pain control versus admissions for treating the underlying condition causing the pain.
- When a neurostimulator is inserted for pain control, the pain code must be assigned as the principal diagnosis regardless of the procedure performed.
- If both an underlying condition treatment and pain control neurostimulator insertion occur, the underlying condition takes precedence as the principal diagnosis.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Assigning the underlying cause of pain as the principal diagnosis when the encounter is solely for pain control or neurostimulator insertion.
- Failing to report the underlying cause of pain as an additional diagnosis when it is known and documented.
- Using G89 codes as secondary diagnoses when the encounter is primarily for pain management without a concurrent underlying condition treatment.
- Omitting the pain code as a secondary diagnosis when a neurostimulator is inserted for pain control alongside treatment of the underlying condition.
- Treating the insertion of a neurostimulator as the principal diagnosis instead of the pain code when the primary reason for admission is pain control.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit acceptance of G89 codes as principal or first-listed diagnosis for pain control encounters.
- Mandatory assignment of pain code as principal diagnosis for neurostimulator insertions aimed at pain control.
- Requirement to prioritize underlying condition codes over pain codes when both are treated in the same encounter.
- Clarification that underlying causes of pain must be reported as additional diagnoses if known.
- Updated guidance on sequencing G89 codes relative to neurostimulator insertion procedures.
