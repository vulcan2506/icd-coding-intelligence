# Hurricane-Related Injury Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Hurricane-Related Injury Coding

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign appropriate external cause codes for injuries not directly caused by the hurricane (e.g., motor vehicle accidents).
- Do not assign code X37.0- for injuries that are not a direct result of the hurricane.
- If the cause is unclear, assume the injury is due to the hurricane and assign code X37.0-.
- Assign applicable external cause codes for exposure to excessive natural heat (X30-), excessive natural cold (X31-), or flood (X38-).
- Combine X37.0- with other applicable external cause codes when multiple factors are present.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Determine if the injury is a direct result of the hurricane before assigning X37.0-.
- If the hurricane is not the direct cause, assign the specific external cause code for the actual mechanism of injury.
- If the hurricane is the direct cause or the cause is unclear, assign X37.0-.
- Include additional external cause codes (X30-, X31-, X38-) if they apply to the specific circumstances of the injury.
- Ensure all assigned codes accurately describe the cause of the morbidity.
