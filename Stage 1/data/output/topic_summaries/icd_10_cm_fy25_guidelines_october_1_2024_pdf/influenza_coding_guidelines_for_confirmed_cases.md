# Influenza Coding Guidelines for Confirmed Cases
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Influenza Coding for Confirmed Cases

**Key Behaviors:**
- Code only confirmed cases of influenza due to certain identified influenza viruses (category J09) or other identified influenza virus (category J10).
- Coding is based on the provider's diagnostic statement of a specific strain (e.g., H1N1, H3N2) rather than requiring positive laboratory testing documentation.
- If the provider records 'suspected', 'possible', or 'probable' influenza, assign category J11 (Influenza due to unidentified influenza virus) instead of J09 or J10.
- Category J09 and J10 codes must not be assigned when the diagnosis is documented as uncertain or probable.
- This guideline serves as an exception to the hospital inpatient guideline regarding uncertain diagnoses.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Provider must document a specific diagnostic statement identifying the influenza strain (e.g., avian influenza, H1N1, H3N2).
- Diagnosis must be confirmed by the provider's statement; laboratory test results are not strictly required for confirmation.
- Uncertain diagnoses (suspected, possible, probable) require assignment of category J11 instead of specific strain categories.
