# Influenza Coding Guidelines for Confirmed Cases
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Influenza Coding for Confirmed Cases

**Key Behaviors:**
- Code only confirmed cases of influenza due to certain identified influenza viruses (category J09) and due to other identified influenza virus (category J10).
- Coding is based on the provider's diagnostic statement regarding specific strains (e.g., avian, H1N1, H3N2) rather than requiring positive laboratory testing documentation.
- If the provider records 'suspected', 'possible', or 'probable' influenza, assign category J11 (Influenza due to unidentified influenza virus) instead of J09 or J10.
- Category J09 should not be assigned if the influenza is not confirmed as due to a certain identified virus.
- Category J10 should not be assigned if the influenza is not confirmed as due to another particular identified strain.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The provider must make a diagnostic statement confirming the patient has a specific identified strain of influenza.
- The diagnosis must be confirmed, not merely suspected, probable, or possible.
- Laboratory testing documentation is not strictly required for confirmation if the provider's statement is sufficient.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Assigning category J09 or J10 codes based solely on 'suspected', 'possible', or 'probable' provider statements.
- Requiring positive laboratory testing documentation as a mandatory condition for assigning J09 or J10 codes.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit exception to the hospital inpatient guideline Section II, H. (Uncertain Diagnosis) for confirmed influenza cases.
- Mandatory use of category J11 for any influenza diagnosis lacking confirmation of a specific identified virus.
