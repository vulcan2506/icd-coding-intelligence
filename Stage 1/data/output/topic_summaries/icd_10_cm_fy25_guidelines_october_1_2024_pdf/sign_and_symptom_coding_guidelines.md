# Sign and Symptom Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Sign and Symptom Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Report codes for signs/symptoms in lieu of a definitive diagnosis if a definitive diagnosis has not been established by the end of the encounter.
- Report appropriate 'unspecified' codes when sufficient clinical information is not known or available to assign a more specific code.
- Report codes for signs and symptoms in addition to a related definitive diagnosis when the sign or symptom is not routinely associated with that diagnosis.
- Sequence the definitive diagnosis code before the symptom code when both are reported.
- Do not assign codes for signs or symptoms that are associated routinely with a disease process as additional codes unless otherwise instructed.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Specific diagnosis codes must be supported by available medical record documentation and clinical knowledge of the patient's health condition.
- The entire medical record must be reviewed to determine the specific reason for the encounter and the conditions treated.
- Coding must reflect the level of certainty known for the encounter.
- It is inappropriate to select a specific code that is not supported by the medical record documentation.
- Conducting medically unnecessary diagnostic testing to determine a more specific code is prohibited.
