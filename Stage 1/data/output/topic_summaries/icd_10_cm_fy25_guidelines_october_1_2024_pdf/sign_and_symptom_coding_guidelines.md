# Sign and Symptom Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Sign and Symptom Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Report codes for signs/symptoms or unspecified codes when a definitive diagnosis has not been established by the end of the encounter.
- Use unspecified codes when sufficient clinical information is unavailable to assign a more specific code, provided they accurately reflect known conditions.
- Report sign/symptom codes in addition to a related definitive diagnosis only when the sign or symptom is not routinely associated with that diagnosis.
- Sequence the definitive diagnosis code before the symptom code when both are reported.
- Do not assign codes for signs or symptoms that are routinely associated with a disease process as additional codes unless otherwise instructed.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Codes must be assigned to the level of certainty known for the specific healthcare encounter.
- Specific diagnosis codes should be reported only when supported by available medical record documentation and clinical knowledge.
- It is inappropriate to select a specific code not supported by the medical record documentation.
- Conducting medically unnecessary diagnostic testing to determine a more specific code is prohibited.
- The entire medical record should be reviewed to determine the specific reason for the encounter and conditions treated.
