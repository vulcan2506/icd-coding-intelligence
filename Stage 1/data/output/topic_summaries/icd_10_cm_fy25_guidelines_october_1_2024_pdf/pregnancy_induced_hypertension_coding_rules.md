# Pregnancy-Induced Hypertension Coding Rules
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Pregnancy-Induced Hypertension Coding Rules

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign code R03.0 for elevated blood pressure reading without a diagnosis of hypertension unless the patient has an established diagnosis of hypertension.
- Assign code O13.- for gestational [pregnancy-induced] hypertension without significant proteinuria.
- Assign code O14.- for pre-eclampsia.
- Codes O13.- and O14.- are designated for transient hypertension of pregnancy.
- Differentiate between transient pregnancy-induced hypertension and established hypertension to select the correct code category.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must confirm the absence of an established hypertension diagnosis to use R03.0.
- Clinical records must specify the presence or absence of significant proteinuria to distinguish between O13.- and O14.-.
- The condition must be confirmed as transient hypertension of pregnancy to utilize O13.- or O14.- codes.
- Established hypertension diagnosis must be documented to preclude the use of R03.0 in favor of chronic hypertension codes.
- Blood pressure readings must be documented as elevated to trigger the application of R03.0 or pregnancy-specific hypertension codes.
