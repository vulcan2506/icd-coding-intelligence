# Factitious Disorder Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Factitious Disorder Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign code F68.1- for patients with documented factitious disorder imposed on self or Munchausen's syndrome.
- Assign code F68.A to the perpetrator's record for Munchausen's syndrome by proxy (MSBP) or factitious disorder imposed on another.
- Assign codes from categories T74 or T76 to the victim of a patient suffering from MSBP.
- Distinguish between the perpetrator (diagnosis recipient) and the victim (maltreatment recipient) in MSBP cases.
- Reference Section I.C.19.f for adult and child abuse, neglect and other maltreatment guidelines.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must confirm the disorder is imposed on self for F68.1- assignment.
- Documentation must identify the perpetrator as the one causing illness in another for F68.A assignment.
- Documentation must confirm or suspect maltreatment for T74 or T76 assignment to the victim.
- The diagnosis must be assigned to the perpetrator's record, not the victim's, in MSBP cases.
- Clinical documentation must support the distinction between factitious disorder and other abuse categories.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Assigning factitious disorder codes to the victim instead of the perpetrator in MSBP cases.
- Using unspecified codes for factitious disorder without specific documentation of self-imposed or other-imposed nature.
- Coding MSBP under general abuse categories without assigning the specific F68.A code to the perpetrator.
- Failing to reference Section I.C.19.f when coding for adult and child abuse, neglect and other maltreatment.
- Assigning factitious disorder codes to patients without documented evidence of false reporting or causing of symptoms.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit use of F68.1- for factitious disorder imposed on self.
- Explicit use of F68.A for factitious disorder imposed on another.
- Mandatory assignment of T74 or T76 codes to victims of MSBP.
- Clear distinction between perpetrator and victim roles in coding MSBP.
- Integration of Section I.C.19.f guidelines for maltreatment coding.
