# HIV Pregnancy Diagnosis Coding Rules
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** HIV Pregnancy Diagnosis Coding Rules

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign principal diagnosis O98.7- for patients admitted due to HIV-related illness during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium.
- Sequence Chapter 15 codes (O98.7-) before HIV-related illness codes (B20) and specific illness codes.
- Assign codes O98.7- and Z21 for patients with asymptomatic HIV infection status during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium.
- Include code(s) for specific HIV-related illness(es) following the principal diagnosis O98.7-.
- Ensure sequencing priority is given to codes from Chapter 15 over other HIV-related codes.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Patient must be admitted or presenting for a healthcare encounter during pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium.
- Clinical documentation must confirm the presence of HIV-related illness or asymptomatic HIV infection status.
- Principal diagnosis must be selected from subcategory O98.7- when HIV complicates the pregnancy period.
- Specific HIV-related illness codes must be included as additional diagnoses following the principal code.
- Asymptomatic status requires the specific combination of O98.7- and Z21 codes.
