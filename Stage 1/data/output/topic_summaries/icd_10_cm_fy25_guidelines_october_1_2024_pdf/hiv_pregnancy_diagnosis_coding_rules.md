# HIV Pregnancy Diagnosis Coding Rules
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** HIV Pregnancy Diagnosis Coding Rules

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign principal diagnosis O98.7- for patients admitted due to HIV-related illness during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium.
- Sequence O98.7- codes before B20 and specific HIV-related illness codes.
- Assign O98.7- and Z21 for patients with asymptomatic HIV infection status during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium.
- Codes from Chapter 15 always take sequencing priority over other conditions.
- Follow specific sequencing rules where O98.7- is principal for symptomatic cases and combined with Z21 for asymptomatic cases.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Patient must be admitted or presenting for a healthcare encounter during pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium.
- Patient must have a documented HIV-related illness for O98.7- principal diagnosis assignment.
- Patient must have documented asymptomatic HIV infection status for Z21 assignment.
- Chapter 15 codes must be sequenced first when applicable.
- Specific HIV-related illness codes must be assigned in addition to O98.7- for symptomatic cases.
