# Secondary Diabetes Medication Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Secondary Diabetes Medication Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign additional Z79 codes to identify long-term use of insulin, oral hypoglycemic drugs, or injectable non-insulin drugs for patients with secondary diabetes mellitus.
- Assign both Z79.4 and Z79.84 when a patient is treated with both oral hypoglycemic drugs and insulin.
- Assign both Z79.4 and Z79.85 when a patient is treated with both insulin and an injectable non-insulin antidiabetic drug.
- Assign both Z79.84 and Z79.85 when a patient is treated with both oral hypoglycemic drugs and an injectable non-insulin antidiabetic drug.
- Do not assign code Z79.4 if insulin is given temporarily to control blood sugar during an encounter.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Patient must have secondary diabetes mellitus.
- Patient must routinely use the specified medication (insulin, oral hypoglycemic drugs, or injectable non-insulin drugs).
- Usage must be long-term (current) rather than temporary.
