# Secondary Diabetes Medication Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Secondary Diabetes Medication Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign additional codes from category Z79 to identify long-term use of insulin, oral hypoglycemic drugs, or injectable non-insulin drugs for patients with secondary diabetes mellitus.
- Assign both Z79.4 and Z79.84 when a patient is treated with both oral hypoglycemic drugs and insulin.
- Assign both Z79.4 and Z79.85 when a patient is treated with both insulin and an injectable non-insulin antidiabetic drug.
- Assign both Z79.84 and Z79.85 when a patient is treated with both oral hypoglycemic drugs and an injectable non-insulin antidiabetic drug.
- Do not assign code Z79.4 if insulin is given temporarily to bring a secondary diabetic patient's blood sugar under control during an encounter.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Patient must have secondary diabetes mellitus.
- Patient must routinely use insulin, oral hypoglycemic drugs, or injectable non-insulin drugs.
- Usage must be long-term (current) to qualify for Z79 codes.
- All applicable medication types used must be coded with their corresponding Z79 subcategory codes.
- Temporary insulin use for blood sugar control during an encounter excludes the assignment of Z79.4.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit requirement to code Z79.85 for long-term use of injectable non-insulin antidiabetic drugs.
- Mandatory combination coding rules for all three drug classes (insulin, oral, injectable non-insulin) when used together.
- Clarification that temporary insulin administration does not constitute long-term use for coding purposes.
