# ICD-10 Pain Code Differentiation
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10 Pain Code Differentiation

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign postoperative pain associated with specific complications to Chapter 19 codes, using G89.18 or G89.28 only if appropriate.
- Reserve Central pain syndrome (G89.0) and chronic pain syndrome (G89.4) for cases where the provider specifically documents these exact conditions.
- Assign pain associated with devices, implants, or grafts to Chapter 19 codes, using specific T codes for device-related pain.
- Use additional codes from category G89 (G89.18 or G89.28) to identify acute or chronic pain due to the presence of a device, implant, or graft.
- Differentiate between general 'chronic pain' terminology and the specific diagnosis of 'chronic pain syndrome' to ensure correct code selection.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Provider must specifically document 'Central pain syndrome' or 'chronic pain syndrome' to use codes G89.0 or G89.4 respectively.
- Pain must be associated with a specific postoperative complication or a device/implant/graft to be assigned to Chapter 19 or T codes.
- Codes G89.18 or G89.28 should be used as additional codes to identify the acute or chronic nature of the pain when applicable.
