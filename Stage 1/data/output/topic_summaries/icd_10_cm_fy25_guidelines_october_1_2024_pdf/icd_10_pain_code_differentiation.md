# ICD-10 Pain Code Differentiation
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10 Pain Code Differentiation

**Key Behaviors:**
- Postoperative pain associated with specific complications is assigned to Chapter 19 codes, with additional G89 codes used only if appropriate.
- Central pain syndrome (G89.0) and chronic pain syndrome (G89.4) require specific provider documentation and are distinct from general 'chronic pain' terms.
- Pain associated with devices, implants, or grafts is assigned to Chapter 19 codes, with additional G89 codes used to identify acute or chronic pain due to the device presence.
- Specific codes for pain due to medical devices are found in the T code section of ICD-10-CM.
- Codes from category G89 (G89.18 or G89.28) are used to identify acute or chronic pain in the context of devices or complications.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Provider must specifically document Central pain syndrome or chronic pain syndrome to use codes G89.0 or G89.4.
- Pain must be explicitly associated with a specific postoperative complication or a device/implant/graft to apply the appropriate coding logic.
- Additional codes from category G89 must be used to identify the nature (acute or chronic) of pain when applicable to complications or devices.
- Reference Section I.C.5 regarding pain disorders related to psychological factors before assigning G89 codes.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Using G89.0 or G89.4 for general 'chronic pain' without specific syndrome documentation.
- Assigning pain associated with complications solely to G89 codes without referencing Chapter 19.
- Failing to use T code section specific codes for pain due to medical devices.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory use of G89.18 or G89.28 to specify acute or chronic pain status for devices and complications.
- Requirement to reference Section I.C.5 for pain disorders related to psychological factors before assigning G89 codes.
