# Bone Site Designation Rules
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Bone Site Designation Rules

**Key Behaviors:**
- For conditions affecting the upper or lower end of a bone (e.g., avascular necrosis, osteoporosis), the site designation must be the bone itself.
- Even if the affected portion of the bone is at the joint, the site designation remains the bone, not the joint.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Clinical documentation must specify the bone affected for conditions like avascular necrosis (M87) or osteoporosis (M80, M81).
- The coder must distinguish between the bone and the joint when the pathology is located at the joint end of the bone.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Coding the site as the joint when the condition specifically affects the bone structure at the joint end.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory assignment of bone site codes over joint codes for specified conditions regardless of anatomical location at the joint.
