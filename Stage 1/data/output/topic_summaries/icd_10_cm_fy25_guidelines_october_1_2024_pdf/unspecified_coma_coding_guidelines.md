# Unspecified Coma Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Unspecified Coma Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign code R40.20 when the underlying cause of the coma is not known.
- Assign code R40.20 when the cause is a traumatic brain injury and the coma scale is not documented.
- Do not report unspecified coma codes for patients with medically induced coma.
- Do not report unspecified coma codes for sedated patients.
- Do not report individual or total Glasgow coma scale scores for medically induced or sedated patients.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The underlying cause of the coma must be unknown to justify R40.20.
- If the cause is traumatic brain injury, the coma scale must be documented in the medical record.
- Documentation must explicitly distinguish between natural coma and medically induced/sedated states.
- Glasgow coma scale scores must be reported only when the patient is not under medical induction or sedation.
- Code R40.20 is invalid if the cause is known or if the patient is in a medically induced coma.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Reporting unspecified coma codes for patients with medically induced coma.
- Reporting unspecified coma codes for sedated patients.
- Reporting individual or total Glasgow coma scale scores for medically induced or sedated patients.
- Assigning R40.20 when the underlying cause of the coma is known.
- Assigning R40.20 for traumatic brain injury when the coma scale is documented.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit prohibition against coding unspecified coma for medically induced or sedated patients.
- Requirement to withhold Glasgow coma scale reporting for medically induced or sedated patients.
- Clarification that R40.20 is reserved for unknown causes or undocumented scales in traumatic brain injury cases.
