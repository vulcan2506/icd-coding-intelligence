# ICD-10-CM Z Code Reporting Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Z Code and External Cause Reporting Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Z codes may be assigned as appropriate to further explain reasons for healthcare encounters, including transfers between facilities.
- Z codes and external cause codes are used to provide additional information relevant to a patient encounter.
- Assign as many codes as necessary to fully explain each healthcare encounter.
- Use any available documentation to assign appropriate external cause of morbidity and Z codes when patient history is limited.
- Z codes and external cause codes are not an all-inclusive list; other codes may be applicable based on documentation.
- Z codes may be assigned as principal/first-listed diagnosis only, secondary diagnosis only, or both depending on circumstances.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Codes must be assigned based on the specific circumstances to determine if they are principal/first-listed or secondary.
- Documentation must support the assignment of specific Z codes (e.g., homelessness, inadequate housing, ventilator dependence).
- Specific Z codes (e.g., Z59.0-, Z59.1, Z59.5, Z75.1, Z75.3, Z75.4, Z76.2, Z99.12) must be assigned based on specific circumstances.
- Assignment of codes must be supported by available documentation, even if patient history is limited.
