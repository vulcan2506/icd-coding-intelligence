# ICD-10-CM Section 21 Factors and Screening
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10-CM Section 21 Factors and Screening

**Key Behaviors:**
- Code factors influencing health status and contact with health services only when they are the reason for the encounter or significantly affect care.
- Assign screening codes only when the patient is asymptomatic and the procedure is performed for the purpose of early detection of a disease or condition.
- Do not code screening results as positive unless the patient has been diagnosed with the condition; use appropriate disease codes instead.
- Ensure documentation explicitly states the intent of the encounter as screening or factor-related to justify code assignment.
- Combine factor codes with relevant screening codes if both conditions are documented as the reason for the encounter.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must clearly identify the factor influencing health status or the specific screening test performed.
- The encounter must be for the purpose of screening, not diagnostic evaluation of symptoms.
- Positive screening results must be documented as a confirmed diagnosis with appropriate disease codes.
- Factors must be clinically relevant to the patient's health status or contact with health services.
- Screening codes should not be used for diagnostic procedures or follow-up of known conditions.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Using screening codes for diagnostic evaluations of symptoms.
- Coding positive screening results without a confirmed diagnosis.
- Assigning factor codes without clinical relevance to the encounter.
- Combining screening codes with diagnostic codes for the same condition without clear distinction.
- Using unspecified codes for screening when specific screening codes are available.

**New in this version:**
- Updated definitions for specific screening tests to align with current medical guidelines.
- Mandatory inclusion of screening test results in clinical documentation for accurate coding.
- Enhanced guidelines for coding factors related to social determinants of health.
- New codes for emerging health factors influencing patient care.
- Clarified rules for coding multiple factors influencing health status in a single encounter.
