# COVID-19 Sequencing Guidelines for Pregnancy
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** COVID-19 Sequencing Guidelines for Pregnancy

**Key Behaviors:**
- Code O98.5- should be sequenced as the principal/first-listed diagnosis when COVID-19 is the reason for admission/encounter during pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium.
- Code U07.1 and codes for associated manifestations should be assigned as additional diagnoses alongside O98.5-.
- Codes from Chapter 15 always take sequencing priority over other codes.
- If the reason for admission is unrelated to COVID-19 but the patient tests positive, the reason for admission code is sequenced first.
- Codes O98.5- and U07.1 are assigned as additional diagnoses when COVID-19 is incidental to the primary reason for admission.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must confirm the patient is in the period of pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium.
- Documentation must confirm COVID-19 is the reason for admission/encounter to sequence O98.5- as principal.
- Documentation must confirm the patient tested positive for COVID-19 during the encounter if it is unrelated to the primary reason for admission.
- Documentation must specify associated manifestations of COVID-19 to assign appropriate additional codes.
- Chapter 15 codes must be prioritized if applicable to the clinical scenario.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory sequencing of O98.5- as the principal diagnosis for COVID-19 during pregnancy/childbirth/puerperium.
- Requirement to assign U07.1 as an additional diagnosis rather than a principal diagnosis in pregnancy contexts.
- Explicit rule that Chapter 15 codes take sequencing priority regardless of pregnancy status.
