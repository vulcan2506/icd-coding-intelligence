# Principal Diagnosis Assignment for Malignancy
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Principal Diagnosis Assignment for Malignancy

**Key Behaviors:**
- Designate the primary malignancy as the principal diagnosis if it is chiefly responsible for the admission and treatment is directed at the primary site.
- Assign the appropriate Z51.-code as the first-listed diagnosis if chemotherapy, immunotherapy, or external beam radiation therapy is chiefly responsible for the admission.
- Sequence the primary site first, followed by any metastatic sites when treating a primary malignancy.
- Do not use symptoms, signs, or ill-defined conditions associated with an existing malignancy to replace the malignancy as the principal diagnosis.
- Query the provider for clarification if the reason for the encounter is unclear regarding the primary site or treatment focus.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The malignancy must be chiefly responsible for occasioning the patient admission or encounter.
- Treatment must be directed at the primary site for the malignancy to be the principal diagnosis.
- Clinical documentation must clearly identify the primary site and the nature of the treatment provided.
- If prophylactic organ removal is the reason for the encounter, specific section I.C.21 guidelines must be followed.
- Symptoms or signs cannot be used as the principal diagnosis if an existing primary or secondary site malignancy is documented.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Using symptoms, signs, or ill-defined conditions to replace the malignancy as the principal diagnosis.
- Assigning the malignancy as the principal diagnosis when chemotherapy or radiation is the chief reason for admission without sequencing Z51.-first.
- Sequencing metastatic sites before the primary site in treatment of a primary malignancy.
- Ignoring the exception for Z51.-codes when administration of systemic therapy is the primary reason for the encounter.
- Failing to sequence the primary site first when multiple sites are involved in the treatment of a primary malignancy.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit requirement to assign Z51.-codes as the first-listed diagnosis when chemotherapy, immunotherapy, or external beam radiation therapy is chiefly responsible for the admission.
- Mandatory sequencing of the primary site first, followed by any metastatic sites, when treating a primary malignancy.
- Prohibition against using symptoms, signs, or ill-defined conditions to replace the malignancy as the principal diagnosis regardless of the number of admissions.
- Reference to specific section I.C.21 for factors influencing health status and contact with health services, including encounter for prophylactic organ removal.
- Requirement to query the provider for clarification if the documentation does not specify the primary site or the chief reason for the encounter.
