# ICD-10-CM Burn and Corrosion Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10-CM Burn and Corrosion Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Distinguish between thermal/electric/radiation burns and chemical corrosions, applying the same guidelines to both.
- Classify burns by depth (first, second, third degree) and site, while classifying eye and internal organ burns by site only.
- Assign separate codes for each burn site unless documentation specifies 'multiple sites' without individual details.
- Use categories T31 or T32 for unspecified sites or to provide additional data on extent of body surface involved.
- Apply the 'rule of nines' for body surface estimation, adjusting percentages for infants, children, or patients with atypical proportions.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must specify the depth, extent, and agent of the burn or corrosion for accurate coding.
- Category T30 (unspecified body region) should rarely be used due to its vagueness.
- Codes from categories T31 and T32 must not be used for sequelae of burns or corrosions.
- When both a current burn and sequelae of an old burn exist, assign both the current code (7th char 'A' or 'D') and the sequelae code (7th char 'S').
- Categories T31 and T32 are mandatory when the site is unspecified or when additional data on body surface involvement is needed for mortality evaluation.
- Documentation must specify the individual sites for burns of 'multiple sites' to allow assignment of separate codes; otherwise, a single code for multiple sites is permitted.
- Documentation must specify the extent of body surface involvement to justify the use of categories T31 or T32 for additional data reporting.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Using Category T30 as a primary code when specific site codes are available.
- Assigning codes from T31 or T32 for sequelae of burns or corrosions.
- Failing to adjust the 'rule of nines' percentages for infants, children, or patients with large buttocks/thighs/abdomens.
- Coding burns of multiple sites with a single code when individual sites are documented.
- Omitting the 7th character 'S' for sequelae when a current burn also exists on the same record.

**New in this version:**
- Explicit distinction between thermal/electric/radiation burns and chemical corrosions in the guidelines.
- Mandatory use of categories T31 and T32 for unspecified sites or when additional data on body surface involvement is required.
- Requirement to adjust 'rule of nines' percentages for specific patient demographics (infants, children, large body parts).
- Allowance for assigning both current burn codes (7th char 'A'/'D') and sequelae codes (7th char 'S') on the same record.
- Guidance to use category T31 as an additional code for reporting purposes when a third-degree burn involves 20% or more of the body surface.
- It is advisable to use category T31 as an additional code for reporting purposes when there is mention of a third-degree burn involving 20 percent or more of the body surface.
