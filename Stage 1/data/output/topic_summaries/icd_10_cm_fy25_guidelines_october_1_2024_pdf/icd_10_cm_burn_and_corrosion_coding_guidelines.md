# ICD-10-CM Burn and Corrosion Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Burn and Corrosion Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Distinguish between thermal/electric/radiation burns and chemical corrosions, applying the same coding guidelines to both.
- Classify burns by depth (first, second, third degree) and site, while classifying eye and internal organ burns by site only.
- Assign separate codes for each burn site unless the medical record documentation does not specify individual sites.
- Use categories T31 or T32 for acute burns/corrosions when the specific site is not specified or additional data is needed for mortality evaluation.
- Apply the 'rule of nines' for estimating body surface area, adjusting percentages for infants, children, or patients with atypical body proportions.
- Assign codes from categories T31 or T32 specifically when the site of the burn/corrosion is not specified OR when there is a need for additional data.
- It is advisable to use category T31 as an additional code when there is mention of a third-degree burn involving 20 percent or more of the body surface.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Codes for 'multiple sites' require that the medical record documentation does not specify the individual sites.
- Categories T31 and T32 must not be used for sequelae of burns or corrosions.
- Categories T31 and T32 are based on the classic 'rule of nines' for estimating body surface involved.
- A code for a current burn/corrosion (7th character 'A' or 'D') and a sequelae code (7th character 'S') may be assigned on the same record if both conditions exist.
- Category T30 (Burn and corrosion, body region unspecified) is extremely vague and should rarely be used.
- Codes for burns of 'multiple sites' should only be assigned when the medical record documentation does not specify the individual sites.
