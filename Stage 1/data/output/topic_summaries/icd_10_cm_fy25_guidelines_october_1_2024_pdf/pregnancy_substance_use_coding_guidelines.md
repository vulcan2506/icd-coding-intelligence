# Pregnancy Substance Use Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Pregnancy Substance Use and Abuse Coding

**Key Behaviors:**
- Assign subcategory O99.31 for alcohol use during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium, followed by a secondary code from category F10.
- Assign subcategory O99.33 for tobacco use during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium, followed by a secondary code from category F17.
- Assign subcategory O99.32 for drug use (illegal or prescription abuse) during pregnancy, childbirth, or puerperium, followed by secondary codes from categories F11-F16 and F18-F19.
- Sequence abuse codes (O9A.3, O9A.4, O9A.5) first for suspected or confirmed abuse cases, followed by codes for associated injuries and perpetrators.
- Ensure secondary codes are assigned to identify specific manifestations or types of dependence for all substance use categories.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must confirm substance use occurred during pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium period.
- Secondary codes from specific categories (F10, F17, F11-F16, F18-F19) must be assigned to identify manifestations.
- For abuse cases, documentation must support the specific type of abuse (physical, sexual, psychological) to justify sequencing O9A codes first.
- Secondary codes must be used to distinguish between different types of nicotine dependence or drug abuse.
- Associated injury codes and perpetrator codes must be included if applicable to abuse cases.
- Documentation must confirm the patient uses any type of tobacco product during pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium to assign O99.33.
- Documentation must confirm the patient uses drugs (including inappropriate use or abuse of prescription drugs) during pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium to assign O99.32.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory assignment of secondary codes from F10, F17, F11-F16, and F18-F19 categories for all substance use complicating pregnancy.
- Specific sequencing rule requiring abuse codes (O9A.3, O9A.4, O9A.5) to be sequenced first before injury or perpetrator codes.
- Inclusion of postpartum period as a valid timeframe for assigning substance use complicating pregnancy codes.
- The guidelines explicitly include 'inappropriate use or abuse of prescription drugs' within the scope of O99.32 Drug use complicating pregnancy, childbirth, and the puerperium.
- The guidelines explicitly include 'any type of tobacco product' within the scope of O99.33 Tobacco use complicating pregnancy, childbirth, and the puerperium.
