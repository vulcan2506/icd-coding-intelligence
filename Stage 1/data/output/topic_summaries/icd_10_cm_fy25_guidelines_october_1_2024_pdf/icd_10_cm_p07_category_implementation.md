# ICD-10-CM P07 Category Implementation
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** ICD-10-CM P07 Category Implementation

**Key Behaviors:**
- Codes from category P07 are for use for a child or adult who was premature or had a low birth weight as a newborn and this is affecting the patient's current health status.
- Codes must be assigned only when the newborn status is affecting the patient's current health status.
- The category applies to individuals who were premature or had low birth weight at the time of birth.
- Codes are utilized for conditions not elsewhere classified under disorders of newborn related to short gestation and low birth weight.
- Documentation must link the current health status directly to the historical newborn condition.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- The patient must have been a newborn who was premature or had a low birth weight.
- The current health status must be demonstrably affected by the historical newborn condition.
- The condition must not be classified elsewhere in the ICD-10-CM classification system.
- Documentation must explicitly state the impact on current health status.
- The code assignment must align with Section I.C.21 regarding factors influencing health status.

**New in this version:**
- Expanded applicability to children and adults for historical newborn conditions affecting current health.
- Mandatory linkage between historical newborn status and current clinical presentation.
- Inclusion of short gestation and low birth weight disorders in the P07 category for ongoing health impacts.
