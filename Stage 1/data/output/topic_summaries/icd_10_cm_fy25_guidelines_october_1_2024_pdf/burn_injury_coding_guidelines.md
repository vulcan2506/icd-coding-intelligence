# Burn Injury Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Burn Injury Coding Guidelines

**Key Behaviors:**
- Sequence first the code that reflects the highest degree of burn when more than one burn is present.
- When a patient has both internal and external burns, the circumstances of admission govern the selection of the principal diagnosis.
- When a patient is admitted for burn injuries and related conditions like smoke inhalation, the circumstances of admission govern the selection of the principal diagnosis.
- Non-healing burns are coded as acute burns.
- Necrosis of burned skin should be coded as a non-healed burn.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Documentation must specify the degree of burn to determine the highest degree for sequencing.
- Clinical documentation must clarify whether the admission reason is for external multiple burns, internal burns, or related conditions.
- Documentation must distinguish between healing and non-healing status of the burn.
- Documentation must identify if necrosis is present in the burned skin.
- The circumstances of admission must be clearly documented to determine the principal diagnosis when multiple conditions exist.
