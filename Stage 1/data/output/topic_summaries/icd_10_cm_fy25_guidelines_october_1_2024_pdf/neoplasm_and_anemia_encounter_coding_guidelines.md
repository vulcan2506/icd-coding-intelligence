# Neoplasm and Anemia Encounter Coding Guidelines
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** Neoplasm and Anemia Encounter Sequencing

**Key Behaviors:**
- When admission is for management of anemia associated with malignancy and treatment is only for anemia, sequence malignancy code first followed by D63.0.
- When admission is for management of a complication associated with neoplasm (e.g., dehydration) and treatment is only for the complication, sequence complication code first followed by neoplasm code(s).
- Anemia associated with malignancy is treated as an exception to the general complication sequencing rule, requiring malignancy as the principal diagnosis.

**Requirements / Properties:**
- Treatment must be limited to anemia management for the malignancy-to-be-first rule to apply.
- Treatment must be limited to the complication management for the complication-to-be-first rule to apply.
- Clinical documentation must explicitly link the anemia to the neoplastic disease.

**Deprecated in this version:**
- Sequencing anemia as the principal diagnosis when the encounter is for management of anemia associated with malignancy.
- Sequencing the neoplasm as the principal diagnosis when the encounter is for management of a complication associated with the neoplasm.

**New in this version:**
- Mandatory use of code D63.0 (Anemia in neoplastic disease) when coding anemia associated with malignancy.
- Explicit exception rule for anemia overriding standard complication sequencing guidelines.
