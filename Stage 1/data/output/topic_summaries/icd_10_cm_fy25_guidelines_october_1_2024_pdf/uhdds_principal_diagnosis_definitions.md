# UHDDS Principal Diagnosis Definitions
*Source: ICD-10-CM FY25 Guidelines October 1, 2024.pdf*

**Feature:** UHDDS Principal Diagnosis Selection

**Key Behaviors:**
- The circumstances of inpatient admission always govern the selection of principal diagnosis.
- Principal diagnosis is defined as the condition established after study to be chiefly responsible for occasioning the admission of the patient to the hospital for care.
- UHDDS definitions apply to all non-outpatient settings including acute care, short term, long term care, psychiatric hospitals, home health, rehab facilities, nursing homes, and hospice services.
- Coding conventions in the ICD-10-CM, Tabular List, and Alphabetic Index take precedence over official coding guidelines when determining principal diagnosis.
- Consistent and complete documentation in the medical record is mandatory for the application of all coding guidelines.

**Mandatory Coding Criteria:**
- Condition must be established after study to be chiefly responsible for the admission.
- Documentation must be consistent and complete in the medical record.
- ICD-10-CM conventions must be prioritized over general coding guidelines for principal diagnosis determination.
